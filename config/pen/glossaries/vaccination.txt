aspiration
    Pulling the syringe plunger before
    intramuscular injection to see if blood is
    aspirated.

    A long standing practice in certain
    geographical areas that was initially
    proposed for safety reasons, i.e. to
    prevent injection in blood vessels. 

reactogenicity
    The property of a vaccine of being able to
    produce common, "expected" adverse
    reactions, especially excessive
    immunological responses and associated
    signs and symptoms, including fever and
    sore arm at the injection site.
