Kia ora Shane,

One of my greatest joys is seeing the Easter message – as it plays out in the daily lives of client whānau and individuals, 365 days a year.

Jesus fully understood the realities of being human. He chose an ultimate act of love, facing down the full extent of our chaos on the cross. And in his resurrection, he ushered in an invitation for us to take part in his generous resurrection life.

Shane, your generosity is revealed as you enable CAP and local churches to walk alongside people like Carlo, who was once facing his own money chaos…

“Anxious, trying to figure out how to get through it… My car finance was quite huge. A credit card as well. Wanting to get out of that hole, but it was difficult. On the mental and emotional side, it was like an itch at the back of my head that was really hard to get rid of.”

Carlo shares the reassurance he felt from his CAP Debt Coach 🙏

“Martha was so welcoming, very funny as well! She was helpful and elaborated on the CAP process. She prayed for me every time, which I’m grateful for.

It’s reassuring to see that the help is there.”

 

Shane, your support is bringing Jesus’ love to people like Carlo – ensuring free practical help and spiritual hope.

“The CAP budget was good. It went from feeling stuck to seeing the end goal in our minds.

Faith-wise, a bit of shift there. I think it's getting stronger, to a degree that my partner goes to church with me as well on a regular basis, and we live and really put into practice what we learn from church as well.”

Carlo reflects on what your support means to him:

“I’m really grateful that you’re enabling people to get out of this downward spiral. We hear that nothing is free in life, but this service is given out of freewill! And putting the word into practice to help one another.”

 
 

And now, Carlo’s hard work has paid off. Today he is free from unamanageable debt and able to plan for the future!

Carlo loves that he and his partner are able to manage their resources, allowing them to invest in time with family, friends, and community. Thanks to you, his ‘downward spiral’ has become an upward and outward spiral!

“We get inspired to do good things for other people, instead of just for ourselves. We think: what about giving back? We want to volunteer our time & resources… to help people in the position we were once in.” — Carlo

Thank you again Shane. Your support expresses God's love to New Zealanders in need in such a meaningful way – it enables people like Carlo to start thriving again! Your generosity reminds me of a scripture in 1 John 4:

“No one has ever seen God. But if we love each other, God lives in us, and his love is brought to full expression in us.” (1 John 4:12 NLT)

In this Easter season, I pray that you know our resurrected and ascended Jesus is near to you; His love making all things new.

 
 

Aroha nui,

 
 

Sam Garaway
CEO, Christians Against Poverty NZ
