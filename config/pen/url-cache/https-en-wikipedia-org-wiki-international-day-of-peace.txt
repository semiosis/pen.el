					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Annual observance dedicated to world peace</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/World_Day_of_Peace" title="World Day of Peace">World Day of Peace</a>.</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output <p>The <b>International Day of Peace</b>, also officially known as <b>World Peace Day</b>, is a <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a>-sanctioned holiday <a href="/wiki/List_of_minor_secular_observances#September" class="mw-redirect" title="List of minor secular observances">observed</a> annually on 21 September. It is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/World_peace" title="World peace">world peace</a>, and specifically the absence of <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">war</a> and <a href="/wiki/Violence" title="Violence">violence</a>, such as might be occasioned by a temporary <a href="/wiki/Ceasefire" title="Ceasefire">ceasefire</a> in a <a href="/wiki/Combat" title="Combat">combat</a> zone for <a href="/wiki/Humanitarian_aid" title="Humanitarian aid">humanitarian aid</a> access. The day was first established in 1981 and first observed in September 1982 and is kept by many nations, <a href="/wiki/Political_groups" class="mw-redirect" title="Political groups">political groups</a>, military groups, and people.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>To inaugurate the day, the United Nations <a href="/wiki/Japanese_Peace_Bell" title="Japanese Peace Bell">Peace Bell</a> is rung at <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Headquarters" class="mw-redirect" title="United Nations Headquarters">UN Headquarters</a> (in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>). The bell is cast from coins donated by children from all continents except <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and was a gift from the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Association" title="United Nations Association">United Nations Association</a> of <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>, as "a reminder of the human cost of war"; the inscription on its side reads, "Long live absolute world peace".<sup id="cite_ref-titleSecretary-General&#39;s_Message_on_the_International_Day_of_Peace_21_September_2002_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-titleSecretary-General&#39;s_Message_on_the_International_Day_of_Peace_21_September_2002-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In recent years, a searchable map of events has been published at un.org.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r886046785">.mw-parser-output .toclimit-2 .toclevel-1 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-3 .toclevel-2 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-4 .toclevel-3 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-5 .toclevel-4 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-6 .toclevel-5 ul,.mw-parser-output .toclimit-7 .toclevel-6 ul{display:none}</style><div class="toclimit-3"><meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" /></div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="1981.E2.80.93_UN_General_Assembly_Resolution_passed"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1981–_UN_General_Assembly_Resolution_passed">1981– UN General Assembly Resolution passed</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 1981– UN General Assembly Resolution passed">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly" title="United Nations General Assembly">United Nations General Assembly</a> declared, in a resolution sponsored by the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-UN_ARES55282_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES55282-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> the International Day of Peace, to be devoted to commemorating and strengthening the ideals of peace.<sup id="cite_ref-UN_ARES3667_page1_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES3667_page1-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The date initially chosen was the regular opening day of the annual sessions of the General Assembly, the third Tuesday of September.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> (This was changed in 2001 to the current annual celebration on 21 September each year&#160;— see 2001 below.)
</p>
<h3><span id="1983_.E2.80.93_Annual_Reports"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1983_–_Annual_Reports">1983 – Annual Reports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 1983 – Annual Reports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beginning in 1983, at the request of the Office of the Secretary-General of the United Nations, Pathways To Peace (PTP) submitted a "We the Peoples" Initiative Annual Report to the UN, summarizing the Peace Day activities.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The reports from 2005 and 2009 are available as archives as cited below. For its initiatives for the International Year of Peace in 1987, PTP was granted "Peace Messenger" status by UN Secretary-General Perez de Cuellar.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
In 2006 the name was changed from "We the Peoples" Initiative to "Culture of Peace Initiative."<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="1996_.E2.80.93_Seanad_.C3.89ireann_debate"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="1996_–_Seanad_Éireann_debate">1996 – Seanad Éireann debate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 1996 – Seanad Éireann debate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A proposal for expanding the International Day of Peace to include Reconciliation,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> in which a massive number of emblems (White Doves) would be distributed after a formal presentation at the United Nations, was put forward by Vincent Coyle, of <a href="/wiki/Derry" title="Derry">Derry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, and was debated at <a href="/wiki/Seanad_%C3%89ireann" title="Seanad Éireann">Seanad Éireann</a>. It was accepted that it would be impractical for one member state to ask for a particular slot at a general UN ceremony.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, events have been held at the United Nations in New York, with the support of Kofi Annan, in April.
</p>
<h3><span id="2001_.E2.80.93_Date_set_at_21_September"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2001_–_Date_set_at_21_September">2001&#160;– Date set at 21 September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 2001 – Date set at 21 September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2001 the opening day of the General Assembly was scheduled for 11 September, and Secretary General Kofi Annan drafted a message recognising the observance of International Peace Day on 21 September.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The September 11 attacks, often referred to as 9/11, were perpetrated on that same day when a series of four coordinated terrorist attacks by the militant Islamist terrorist group al-Qaeda against the United States of America occurred just blocks away from the UN on the morning of Tuesday, September 11, 2001. That year the day was changed from the third Tuesday to specifically the twenty-first day of September, to take effect in 2002. A new resolution was passed by the General Assembly,<sup id="cite_ref-UN_ARES55282_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UN_ARES55282-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> sponsored by the United Kingdom (giving credit to <a href="/wiki/Peace_One_Day" title="Peace One Day">Peace One Day</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">Costa Rica</a> (the original sponsors of the day), to give the International Day of Peace a fixed calendar date, 21 September, and declare it also as a day of <a href="/wiki/Ceasefire" title="Ceasefire">global ceasefire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Non-violence" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-violence">non-violence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2004_.E2.80.93_Taiwanese_commemorative_stamp_controversy"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2004_–_Taiwanese_commemorative_stamp_controversy">2004 – Taiwanese commemorative stamp controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2004 – Taiwanese commemorative stamp controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A diplomatic stir occurred when <a href="/wiki/Lions_Clubs_International" title="Lions Clubs International">Lions Clubs International</a> sponsored a competition for six posters to be used for International Day of Peace commemorative stamps issued by the UN Postal Administration. A poster by 15-year-old <a href="/wiki/Taiwanese_people" title="Taiwanese people">Taiwanese</a> school student Yang Chih-Yuan was announced as one of the winners, but the announcement was withdrawn. Taiwan media reports, Taiwan Lions Club and the government of Taiwan claimed the decision not to use the poster resulted from pressure from <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="People&#39;s Republic of China">China</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-TaipeiTimes_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TaipeiTimes-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> the rejection of the student's painting on political grounds did not reflect the ideals of the International Day of Peace.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The UN issued a statement that, although in the shortlist of eight designs, "due to an internal misunderstanding and miscommunication, Mr. Yang's proof got publicized in error as one of the six stamps intended to be issued."<sup id="cite_ref-TaipeiTimes_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TaipeiTimes-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The government of <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of China">Taiwan (Republic of China)</a> later issued a stamp containing the image.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2005_.E2.80.93_UN_Secretary_General_calls_for_22-hour_ceasefire"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2005_–_UN_Secretary_General_calls_for_22-hour_ceasefire">2005&#160;– UN Secretary General calls for 22-hour ceasefire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: 2005 – UN Secretary General calls for 22-hour ceasefire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<p>In 2006, then Secretary-General Kofi Annan rang the Peace Bell for the last time during his term in office. That year the UN asserted the "many ways it works for peace and to encourage individuals, groups and communities around the world to contemplate and communicate thoughts and activities on how to achieve peace." The United Kingdom held the primary public and official observation of the United Nations International Day of Peace and Non-Violence in <a href="/wiki/Rochdale" title="Rochdale">Rochdale</a>, Greater Manchester, organized by <a href="/wiki/Peace_Parade_UK" title="Peace Parade UK">Peace Parade UK</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-titleInternational_Day_of_Peace_2006_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-titleInternational_Day_of_Peace_2006-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="2011_.E2.80.93_Peace_and_Democracy:_Make_Your_Voice_Heard"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2011_–_Peace_and_Democracy:_Make_Your_Voice_Heard">2011 – Peace and Democracy: Make Your Voice Heard</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 2011 – Peace and Democracy: Make Your Voice Heard">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2011 the UN Peace Day's theme was "Peace and Democracy: Make Your Voice Heard". Many organizations held Peace Day events worldwide in 2011. Organizations like <a href="/wiki/Peace_One_Day" title="Peace One Day">Peace One Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wiser.org" title="Wiser.org">Wiser</a> and <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Peace" title="Culture of Peace">Culture of Peace</a> have been active participants in Peace Day activities for years.

<h3><span id="2012_.E2.80.93_Sustainable_Peace_for_a_Sustainable_Future"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_–_Sustainable_Peace_for_a_Sustainable_Future">2012&#160;– Sustainable Peace for a Sustainable Future</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 2012 – Sustainable Peace for a Sustainable Future">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2012, the United Nations set the theme for the year's observance as Sustainable Peace for a Sustainable Future, commemorating and strengthening the ideals of peace both within and among all nations and peoples.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>

<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Global_Truce_Day_2012">Global Truce Day 2012</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Global Truce Day 2012">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2011, <a href="/wiki/Peace_One_Day" title="Peace One Day">Peace One Day</a> announced at their <a href="/wiki/O2_Arena_(London)" class="mw-redirect" title="O2 Arena (London)">O2 Arena</a> concert, a new international campaign called <i><a href="/wiki/Peace_One_Day#Global_Truce_2012" title="Peace One Day">Global Truce 2012</a></i>, a <a href="/wiki/Grassroots" title="Grassroots">grassroots</a> initiative and international coalition with <a href="/wiki/Non-governmental_organisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-governmental organisation">non-governmental organisations</a> and <a href="/wiki/Students%27_union" title="Students&#39; union">Students' unions</a> in every continent, which increased participation and action on Peace Day 2012, the day of <i>Global Truce</i>. Particular focus in this campaign included a <a href="/wiki/Cessation_of_hostilities" class="mw-redirect" title="Cessation of hostilities">cessation of hostilities</a> on the day and a reduction of <a href="/wiki/Domestic_violence" title="Domestic violence">domestic violence</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bullying" title="Bullying">bullying</a> in society. The <i>Peace One Day Celebration</i> concert on Peace Day in 2012 was held at <a href="/wiki/Wembley_Arena" title="Wembley Arena">Wembley Arena</a> to celebrate <i>Global Truce 2012</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Global_Truce_Concert_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Global_Truce_Concert-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The <i>Global Truce</i> campaign will continue and be named with each year it leads up to, involving more partners and coalitions for mass participation and <a href="/wiki/Lifesaving" title="Lifesaving">life-saving</a> practical action on Peace Day.
</p>

<p>The theme of the 2015 International Day of Peace was "Partnerships for Peace – <a href="/wiki/Dignity" title="Dignity">Dignity</a> for All".<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span id="2016_.E2.80.93_The_Sustainable_Development_Goals:_Building_Blocks_for_Peace"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016_–_The_Sustainable_Development_Goals:_Building_Blocks_for_Peace">2016 – The Sustainable Development Goals: Building Blocks for Peace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: 2016 – The Sustainable Development Goals: Building Blocks for Peace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The theme of the 2016 International Day of Peace was "The <a href="/wiki/Sustainable_Development_Goals" title="Sustainable Development Goals">Sustainable Development Goals</a>: Building Blocks for Peace".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2017_.E2.80.93_Together_for_Peace:_Respect.2C_Safety_and_Dignity_for_All"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017_–_Together_for_Peace:_Respect,_Safety_and_Dignity_for_All">2017 – Together for Peace: Respect, Safety and Dignity for All</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: 2017 – Together for Peace: Respect, Safety and Dignity for All">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>This theme was based on the TOGETHER global campaign that promotes <a href="/wiki/Respect" title="Respect">respect</a>, safety and dignity for everyone forced to flee their homes in search of a <a href="/wiki/Quality_of_life" title="Quality of life">better life</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Peace_Crane_Project">The Peace Crane Project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: The Peace Crane Project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In 2017, <a href="/wiki/The_Peace_Crane_Project" class="mw-redirect" title="The Peace Crane Project">The Peace Crane Project</a> announced the goal of collecting 1,000 cranes from students around the world to display in various venues to celebrate.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2017_Global_survey_of_celebration">2017 Global survey of celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: 2017 Global survey of celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A survey by the <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Peace_News_Network" title="Culture of Peace News Network">Culture of Peace News Network</a> found internet reports about 562 celebrations of the International Day of Peace from 127 countries around the world this year.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> These included 128 events coming from most of the provinces and states in Canada and the USA. Next were the countries formerly part of the Soviet Union with 104. There were 96 events cited in 27 European countries, 81 from 29 African countries, 67 from 20 Asian countries, 58 from 16 Latin American and Caribbean countries, and 28 from 21 Arab and Middle Eastern countries.
</p>
<h3><span id="2018_.E2.80.93_The_Right_to_Peace_.E2.80.93_The_Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights_at_70"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_–_The_Right_to_Peace_–_The_Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights_at_70">2018 – The Right to Peace – The Universal Declaration of Human Rights at 70</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: 2018 – The Right to Peace – The Universal Declaration of Human Rights at 70">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 2018 U.N. Peace Day Theme was "The Right to Peace – The Universal Declaration of Human Rights at 70."<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2018_Global_survey_of_celebration">2018 Global survey of celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: 2018 Global survey of celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A survey by the <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Peace_News_Network" title="Culture of Peace News Network">Culture of Peace News Network</a> found internet reports about 764 celebrations of the International Day of Peace from 129 countries around the world this year.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> These included 233 events coming from most of the provinces and states in Canada and the USA. Next were 177 events from Europe and 158 events from Asia. There were 95 events from Latin America and the Caribbean, 71 events from countries formerly part of the Soviet Union, 71 from Africa, and 15 from Arab and Middle Eastern countries.
</p>
<h3><span id="2019_.E2.80.93_Climate_Action_for_Peace"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2019_–_Climate_Action_for_Peace">2019 – Climate Action for Peace</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: 2019 – Climate Action for Peace">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The United Nations selected the theme "Climate Action for Peace" for the 2019 International Day of Peace.
</p><p>According to the UN website, "The United Nations calls upon all to take action to tackle climate change."<sup id="cite_ref-:0_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>"On 23 September [2019], the United Nations is convening a Climate Action Summit with concrete and realistic plans to accelerate <a href="/wiki/Individual_and_political_action_on_climate_change" class="mw-redirect" title="Individual and political action on climate change">action</a> to implement the <a href="/wiki/Paris_Agreement" title="Paris Agreement">Paris Agreement</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The International Day of Peace Student Observance on 20 September 2019 at United Nations Headquarters featured young people presenting their projects to fight climate change and promote peace.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_46-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2019_Global_survey_of_celebration">2019 Global survey of celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: 2019 Global survey of celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A survey by the <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Peace_News_Network" title="Culture of Peace News Network">Culture of Peace News Network</a> found internet reports concerning more than 655 celebrations of the International Day of Peace from 103 countries around the world in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2020_.E2.80.93_Shaping_Peace_Together"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_–_Shaping_Peace_Together">2020 – Shaping Peace Together</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: 2020 – Shaping Peace Together">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The United Nations has selected the theme "Shaping Peace Together" for the 2020 International Day of Peace.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the UN website, "This year, it has been clearer than ever that we are not each other's enemies. Rather, our common enemy is a <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">tireless virus</a> that threatens our health, security and very way of life. COVID-19 has thrown our world into turmoil and forcibly reminded us that what happens in one part of the planet can impact people everywhere."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2020_Global_survey_of_celebration">2020 Global survey of celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=35" title="Edit section: 2020 Global survey of celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A survey by the <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Peace_News_Network" title="Culture of Peace News Network">Culture of Peace News Network</a> found internet reports concerning more than 717 celebrations of the International Day of Peace from 78 countries around the world this year.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> The largest number came from Western Europe<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> and from the European countries formerly part of the Soviet Union.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2021_.E2.80.93_Recovering_Better_for_an_Equitable_and_Sustainable_World"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2021_–_Recovering_Better_for_an_Equitable_and_Sustainable_World">2021 – Recovering Better for an Equitable and Sustainable World</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=36" title="Edit section: 2021 – Recovering Better for an Equitable and Sustainable World">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The 2021 theme for the International Day of Peace was "Recovering Better for an Equitable and Sustainable World.'"<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="2021_Global_survey_of_celebration">2021 Global survey of celebration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Day_of_Peace&amp;action=edit&amp;section=37" title="Edit section: 2021 Global survey of celebration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>A survey by the <a href="/wiki/Culture_of_Peace_News_Network" title="Culture of Peace News Network">Culture of Peace News Network</a> found internet reports concerning more than 628 celebrations of the International Day of Peace from 79 countries around the world in 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
