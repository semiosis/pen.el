<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Background">Background</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_of_Cyrene&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Cyrene,_Libya" title="Cyrene, Libya">Cyrene</a> was a Greek city in the province of <a href="/wiki/Cyrenaica" title="Cyrenaica">Cyrenaica</a>, in eastern <a href="/wiki/Libya" title="Libya">Libya</a>, in northern Africa. It had a Jewish community, where 100,000 <a href="/wiki/Judean" class="mw-redirect" title="Judean">Judean</a> Jews settled during the reign of <a href="/wiki/Ptolemy_Soter" class="mw-redirect" title="Ptolemy Soter">Ptolemy Soter</a> (323–285 BC), and was an <a href="/wiki/Early_centers_of_Christianity#Cyrene" class="mw-redirect" title="Early centers of Christianity">early center of Christianity</a>.
</p><p>The Cyrenian Jews had a <a href="/wiki/Synagogue" title="Synagogue">synagogue</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>, where many went for annual feasts.<sup id="cite_ref-Today_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Today-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Biblical_accounts">Biblical accounts</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_of_Cyrene&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Biblical accounts"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Simon's act of carrying the cross, <i><a href="/wiki/Crucifixion#Details" title="Crucifixion">patibulum</a></i> (crossbeam in Latin), for Jesus is the fifth station of the <a href="/wiki/Stations_of_the_Cross" title="Stations of the Cross">Stations of the Cross</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>6<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some interpret the passage as indicating that Simon was chosen because he may have shown sympathy with Jesus.<sup id="cite_ref-Today_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Today-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Others point out that the text itself says nothing, that he had no choice, and that there is no basis to consider the carrying of the cross an act of sympathetic generosity.<sup id="cite_ref-Matthew_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Matthew-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Mark_15:21" class="mw-redirect" title="Mark 15:21">Mark 15:21</a> identifies Simon as "the father of Alexander and Rufus". Tradition states that they became <a href="/wiki/Missionary" title="Missionary">missionaries</a>; the inclusion of their names may suggest that they were of some standing in the <a href="/wiki/Early_centers_of_Christianity#Rome" class="mw-redirect" title="Early centers of Christianity">Early Christian community at Rome</a>. Mark's Gospel, which was written for a Roman audience, seems to suggest that the audience knew who these men were. It has also been suggested that the Rufus (in Greek: Ῥοῦφον or <i>Rhouphon</i>) mentioned by Paul in <a href="/wiki/Epistle_to_the_Romans" title="Epistle to the Romans">Romans</a> <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Bible_(King_James)/Romans#16:13" class="extiw" title="s:Bible (King James)/Romans">16:13</a> is the son of Simon of Cyrene.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>8<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Some also link Simon himself with the "men of Cyrene" who preached the Gospel to the <a href="/wiki/Hellenized_Jews" class="mw-redirect" title="Hellenized Jews">Hellenized Jews</a> (<a href="/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%B5%CE%BB%CE%BB%CE%B7%CE%BD%CE%B9%CF%83%CF%84%CE%AE%CF%82" class="extiw" title="wiktionary:ελληνιστής">Ελληνιστάς</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>9<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> in <a href="/wiki/Acts_of_the_Apostles" title="Acts of the Apostles">Acts</a> <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Bible_(King_James)/Acts#11:20" class="extiw" title="s:Bible (King James)/Acts">11:20</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Today_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Today-5"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>5<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> On the other hand, Simon's name alone does not prove he was Jewish, and Alexander and Rufus were both common names and may have referred to others.<sup id="cite_ref-Matthew_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Matthew-7"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>7<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>A burial cave in the <a href="/wiki/Kidron_Valley" title="Kidron Valley">Kidron Valley</a> discovered in 1941 by <a href="/wiki/Eleazar_Sukenik" title="Eleazar Sukenik">E. L. Sukenik</a>, belonging to Cyrenian Jews and dating before AD 70, was found to have an ossuary inscribed twice in Greek "Alexander son of Simon". It cannot, however, be certain that this refers to the same person.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>10<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>11<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Gnostic_and_Islamic_views">Gnostic and Islamic views</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_of_Cyrene&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Gnostic and Islamic views"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Gnostic">Gnostic</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_of_Cyrene&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Gnostic"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>According to some <a href="/wiki/Gnostic" class="mw-redirect" title="Gnostic">Gnostic</a> traditions, Simon of Cyrene, by mistaken identity, suffered the events leading up to the crucifixion. This is the story presented in the <a href="/wiki/Second_Treatise_of_the_Great_Seth" title="Second Treatise of the Great Seth">Second Treatise of the Great Seth</a>, although it is unclear whether Simon or another actually died on the cross.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>12<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> This is part of a belief held by some Gnostics that Jesus was not of flesh, but only took on the appearance of flesh (see also <a href="/wiki/Basilides" title="Basilides">Basilides</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Swoon_hypothesis" title="Swoon hypothesis">Swoon hypothesis</a>).
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Basilides" title="Basilides">Basilides</a>, in his <a href="/wiki/Gospel_of_Basilides" title="Gospel of Basilides">gospel of Basilides</a>, is reported by <a href="/wiki/Irenaeus" title="Irenaeus">Irenaeus</a> as having taught a <a href="/wiki/Docetic" class="mw-redirect" title="Docetic">docetic</a> doctrine of Christ's passion. He states the teaching that Christ, in Jesus, as a wholly divine being, could not suffer bodily pain and did not die on the cross; but that the person crucified was, in fact, Simon of Cyrene.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>13<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>14<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Irenaeus quotes Basiledes:
</p>
<blockquote><p>He appeared on earth as a man and performed miracles. Thus he himself did not suffer. Rather, a certain Simon of Cyrene was compelled to carry his cross for him. It was he who was ignorantly and erroneously crucified, being transfigured by him, so that he might be thought to be Jesus.  Moreover, Jesus assumed the form of Simon, and stood by laughing at them.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>15<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup><span style="white-space: nowrap;">&#8201;—&#8201;</span>Irenaeus, <a href="/wiki/On_the_Detection_and_Overthrow_of_the_So-Called_Gnosis" class="mw-redirect" title="On the Detection and Overthrow of the So-Called Gnosis"><i>Against Heresies</i></a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>16<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup></p></blockquote>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Islamic">Islamic</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_of_Cyrene&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Islamic"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1236090951">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}@media print{body.ns-0 .mw-parser-output .hatnote{display:none!important}}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Islamic_view_of_Jesus%27_death" class="mw-redirect" title="Islamic view of Jesus&#39; death">Islamic view of Jesus' death</a></div><p>Orthodox Muslim theology teaches Jesus <a href="/wiki/Entering_heaven_alive" title="Entering heaven alive">ascended to Heaven</a> without dying on the cross.
</p><div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="In_popular_culture">In popular culture</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_of_Cyrene&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: In popular culture"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>According to the visions of <a href="/wiki/Anne_Catherine_Emmerich" title="Anne Catherine Emmerich">Anne Catherine Emmerich</a>, Simon was a <a href="/wiki/Paganism" title="Paganism">pagan</a>. The Romans recognized he was not a Jew by his clothes and then chose him to oblige him to help Jesus carry the cross.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>17<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>Poet <a href="/wiki/Ridgely_Torrence" title="Ridgely Torrence">Ridgely Torrence</a> wrote a play about him titled <i>Simon the Cyrenian</i>. A 1920 <a href="/wiki/YWCA" title="YWCA">YWCA</a> production of this play was directed by Dora Cole, sister of composer <a href="/wiki/Bob_Cole_(composer)" title="Bob Cole (composer)">Bob Cole</a>, and starred <a href="/wiki/Paul_Robeson" title="Paul Robeson">Paul Robeson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>18<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Sidney_Poitier" title="Sidney Poitier">Sidney Poitier</a> was cast as Simon of Cyrene in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Greatest_Story_Ever_Told" title="The Greatest Story Ever Told">The Greatest Story Ever Told</a></i> that was directed by <a href="/wiki/George_Stevens" title="George Stevens">George Stevens</a> and released in 1965.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>19<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
</p><p>In the 1979 comedy film <i><a href="/wiki/Monty_Python%27s_Life_of_Brian" title="Monty Python&#39;s Life of Brian">Monty Python's Life of Brian</a></i> is a vignette alluding to Simon of Cyrene. A seemingly pious and generous man offers to one of the condemned carrying a cross, "Brother, let me shoulder your burden." Upon doing so, the condemned man runs off, leaving the generous man stuck with the cross and future crucifixion.
</p><p>The film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Passion_of_the_Christ" title="The Passion of the Christ">The Passion of the Christ</a></i> portrays Simon (<a href="/w/index.php?title=Jarreth_Merz&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jarreth Merz (page does not exist)">Jarreth Merz</a>) as a Jew who, having been forced by the Romans to carry the cross, is initially unwilling but comes to show compassion to Jesus and helps him.
</p><p>French singer-poet <a href="/wiki/Georges_Brassens" title="Georges Brassens">Georges Brassens</a> mentioned Simon in one of the verses of his famous song-poem <a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Pri%C3%A8re" class="extiw" title="fr:La Prière"><i>La prière</i></a> (The prayer): "<i>Comme la croix du fils sur Simon de Cyrène</i>" (Like the son's cross on Simon of Cyrene). The song is based on an original poem called <i>Rosaire</i> by French poet <a href="/wiki/Francis_Jammes" title="Francis Jammes">Francis Jammes</a> that also contains this verse.
</p>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Movements">Movements</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_of_Cyrene&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Movements"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Both the <a href="/wiki/Simon_Community" title="Simon Community">Simon Community</a>, and the Cyrenian movement (which provides services to homeless and other disadvantaged groups in the UK)<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>20<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> take their name from Simon of Cyrene.
</p>
