        <h2 class="element-invisible"><a href="/file/28-biblical-passages-teach-there-only-one-god">28 Biblical passages that teach there is only one God</a></h2>
    
  
  <div class="content">
    <a href="https://mit.irr.org/files/files/Images/28-biblical-passages.jpg-1.jpg" title="28 Biblical passages that teach there is only one God" class="colorbox" data-colorbox-gallery="gallery-file-3267-YH0WjBVleXQ" data-cbox-img-attrs="{&quot;title&quot;: &quot;28 Biblical passages that teach there is only one God&quot;, &quot;alt&quot;: &quot;An open Bible to show passages that teach only one God&quot;}"><img typeof="foaf:Image" src="https://mit.irr.org/files/files/Images/28-biblical-passages.jpg-1.jpg" width="700" height="403" alt="An open Bible to show passages that teach only one God" title="28 Biblical passages that teach there is only one God" /></a>  </div>

  
</div>
</div></div></div><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><p style="padding-top: 5px; padding-bottom: 5px; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 5px;">There are not a few ambiguous Biblical passages that teach that there is only one God, but many explicit passages that clearly declare this cardinal truth. Each of the following 28 passages explicitly teach that there is one — and only one — true and living God. The Mormon plurality of Gods doctrine (found most explicitly in the Pearl of Great Price/Book of Abraham 4-5) contradicts this fundamental biblical teaching.</p>
<div class="pullquote" style="border-right-width: 0px; border-left-width: 0px; border-style: initial; border-color: initial;">"The Mormon plurality of Gods doctrine (found most explicitly in the Pearl of Great Price/Book of Abraham 4-5) contradicts this fundamental biblical teaching."</div>
<p style="padding-top: 5px; padding-bottom: 5px; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 5px;">Please note that the King James Version (used here) designates the Hebrew name Jehovah (or Yahweh) with "LORD" spelled with all letters capitalized; Adonai, meaning "Lord" or "Master," is designated by "Lord;" and "Elohim," is translated as "God." Thus, in Deut. 4:35, for example, the phrase "the LORD he is God," would be literally translated, "Jehovah, he is Elohim." (In those instances were Adonai is used as a compound with Jehovah, the latter term is spelled GOD, so that the compound name "Lord GOD" designates the Hebrew, "Adonai Jehovah").<br /><br /><strong>1. Deuteronomy 4:35,39 </strong>— Unto thee it was shown, that thou mightest know that the LORD he is God; <strong>there is none else beside him</strong>. (39) Know therefore this day, and consider it in thine heart, that the LORD he is God in heaven above, and upon the earth beneath: there is none else.<br /><br /><strong>2. Deuteronomy 6:4</strong> — Hear, O Israel: <strong>The LORD thy God is one LORD</strong>. [Note in Mark 12:28-34 how Jesus and a Jewish scribe he encountered understood this text.]<br /><br /><strong>3. Deuteronomy32:39</strong> — See now that I, even I, am he, and <strong>there is no god with me</strong>: I kill, and I make alive; I wound, and I heal: neither is there any that can deliver out of my hand.<br /><br /><strong>4. 2 Samuel 7:22</strong> — Wherefore thou art great, O LORD God; for there is none like thee, <strong>neither is there any God beside thee</strong>, according to all that we have heard with our ears.<br /><br /><strong>5. 1 Kings 8:60</strong> — That all the people of the earth may know that the LORD is God, <strong>and that there is none else.</strong><br /><br /><strong>6. 2 KINGS 5:15</strong> — And he returned to the man of God, he and all his company, and came, and stood before him: and he said, Behold, now I know that <strong>there is no God in all the earth, but in Israel;</strong> now therefore, I pray thee, take a blessing of thy servant.<br /><br /><strong>7. 2 Kings 19:15</strong> — And Hezekiah prayed before the LORD, and said, O LORD God of Israel, which dwellest between the cherubims, thou art the God, <strong>even thou alone</strong>, of all the kingdoms of the earth; thou hast made heaven and earth.</p>
<p style="padding-top: 5px; padding-bottom: 5px; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 5px;"><span class="style1" style="background-color: transparent; border-style: initial; border-color: initial; text-align: left; font-family: 'Times New Roman'; border-width: 0px; padding: 0px; margin: 0px;"><strong>8</strong></span><strong>. 1 Chronicles 17:20</strong> — O LORD, there is none like thee, <strong>neither is there any God beside thee</strong>, according to all that we have heard with our ears.<br /><br /><strong>9. Nehemiah 9:6 </strong>— Thou, even <strong>thou, art LORD alone</strong>; thou has made heaven, the heaven of heavens, with all their host, the earth, and all things that are therein, the seas, and all that is therein, and thou preservest them all; and the host of heaven worshippeth thee.<br /><br /><strong>10. Psalm 18:31 </strong>— For <strong>who is God save the LORD? </strong>or who is a rock save our God?<br /><br /><strong>11. Psalm 86:10</strong> — For thou art great, and doest wondrous things: <strong>thou art God alone.<br /></strong><br /><strong>12. Isaiah 37:16,20</strong> — O LORD of hosts, God of Israel, that dwellest between the cherubims, thou art the God, <strong>even thou alon</strong>e, of all the kingdoms of the earth: thou has made heaven and earth. (20) Now therefore, O LORD our God, save us from his hand, that all the kingdoms of the earth may know that thou art the LORD, even thou only.<br /><br /><strong>13. Isaiah43:10,11</strong> — Ye are my witnesses, saith the LORD, and my servant whom I have chosen: that ye may know and believe me, and understand that I am he:<strong>before me there was no God formed, neither shall there be after me</strong>. I, even I, am the LORD; <strong>and beside me there is no savior.</strong><br /><br /><strong>14. Isaiah44:6,8 </strong>— Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer the LORD of hosts;<strong> I am the first, and</strong> <strong>I am the last; and beside me there is no God.</strong>Fear ye not, neither be afraid; have not I told thee from that time, and have declared it? ye are even my witnesses. Is there a God beside me? yea, there is no God; I know not any.<br /><br /><strong>15. Isaiah 45:21 </strong>— Tell ye, and bring them near; yea, let them take counsel together: who hath declared this from ancient time: who hath told it from that time? have not I the LORD? and <strong>there is no God else beside me</strong>; a just God and a Savior; there is none beside me.<br /><br /><strong>16. Isaiah 46:9</strong> — For I am God, and <strong>there is none else; I am God, and there is none like me.<br /></strong><br /><strong>17. Hosea 13:4 </strong>— Yet I am the LORD thy God from the land of Egypt, and thou shalt know no god but me; for <strong>there is no savior beside me.</strong><br /><br /><strong>18. Joel 2:27 </strong>— And ye shall know that I am in the midst of Israel, and that I<strong> am the LORD your God, and none else</strong>: and my people shall never be ashamed.<br /><br /><strong>19. Zechariah 14:9 </strong>— And the LORD shall be king over all the earth: in that day shall there be one Lord, and his name one.<br /><br /><strong>20. Mark 12:29-34</strong> —And Jesus answered him, The first of all the commandments is, Hear, O Israel; <strong>The Lord our God is one Lord</strong>: And thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy mind, and with all thy strength: this is the first commandment. And the second is like, namely this, thou shalt love thy neighbor as thyself. There is none other commandment greater than these. And the scribe said unto him, Well, Master, thou hast said the truth: <strong>for there is one God; and there is none other but he:</strong> And to love him with all the heart, and with all the understanding, and with all the soul, and with all the strength, and to love his neighbor as himself, is more than all whole burnt offerings and sacrifices. And when Jesus saw that he answered discreetly, he said unto him, Thou art not far from the kingdom of God. And no man after that durst ask him any question.<br /><br /><strong>21. John 17:3</strong> — And this is life eternal, that they might know thee t<strong>he only true God,</strong> and Jesus Christ, whom thou hast sent.<br /><br /><strong>22. Romans 3:30</strong> — Seeing it is one God, which shall justify the circumcision by faith, and uncircumcision through faith.<br /><br /><strong>23. 1 Corinthians 8:4-6</strong> — As concerning therefore the eating of those things that are offered in sacrifice unto idols, we know that an idol is nothing in the world, and<strong>that there is none other God but one</strong>. For though there be that are called gods, whether in heaven or in earth, (as there be gods many, and lords many,) <strong>But to us there is but one God</strong>, the Father, of whom all things, and we in him; and one Lord Jesus Christ, by whom are all things, and we by him.<br /><br /><strong>24. Galatians 3:20</strong> — Now a mediator is not a mediator of one, <strong>but God is one.</strong><br /><br /><strong>25. Ephesians 4:6</strong> — <strong>One God and Father of all</strong>, who is above all, and through all, and in you all.</p>
<p style="padding-top: 5px; padding-bottom: 5px; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 5px;"><strong>26. 1 Timothy 1:17 </strong>— Now unto the King eternal, immortal, invisible, <strong>the only wise God</strong>, be honour and glory for ever and ever. Amen.</p>
<p style="padding-top: 5px; padding-bottom: 5px; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 5px;"><strong>27. 1 Timothy 2:5 </strong>— <strong>For there is one God, </strong>and one mediator between God and men, the man Christ Jesus.</p>
<p style="padding-top: 5px; padding-bottom: 5px; margin-top: 0px; margin-bottom: 5px;"><strong>28. James 2:19 </strong>— Thou believest that t<strong>here is one God;</strong> thou doest well: the devils also believe, and tremble.<br /> </p>
