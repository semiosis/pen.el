					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content"><div class="mw-content-ltr mw-parser-output" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Valley mentioned in the Bible</div>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Historische_Karte_von_Jerusalem_MK1888.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Historische_Karte_von_Jerusalem_MK1888.png/220px-Historische_Karte_von_Jerusalem_MK1888.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="233" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Historische_Karte_von_Jerusalem_MK1888.png/330px-Historische_Karte_von_Jerusalem_MK1888.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c3/Historische_Karte_von_Jerusalem_MK1888.png/440px-Historische_Karte_von_Jerusalem_MK1888.png 2x" data-file-width="739" data-file-height="782" /></a><figcaption>In this historic map of biblical Jerusalem, the Cedron valley (German <i>Kidron Thal oder Josaphat Thal</i> – Kidron Valley or Josaphat Valley) runs along the eastern wall of the  city (<i><a href="/wiki/Meyers_Konversationslexikon" class="mw-redirect" title="Meyers Konversationslexikon">Meyers Konversationslexikon</a></i>, 1885).</figcaption></figure>
<p>The <b>Valley of Josaphat</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">Hebrew</a>: <span lang="he" dir="rtl">עמק יהושפט</span>, <small><a href="/wiki/Romanization_of_Hebrew" title="Romanization of Hebrew">romanized</a>:&#160;</small><span title="Hebrew-language romanization"><i lang="he-Latn">‘Êmeq Yəhōšāp̄āṭ</i></span>; variants: <b>Valley of Jehoshaphat</b> and <b>Valley of Yehoshephat</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Biblical" class="mw-redirect" title="Biblical">Biblical</a> place mentioned by name in the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Joel" title="Book of Joel">Book of Joel</a> (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://bible.oremus.org/?passage=Joel%203:2&amp;version=nrsv">Joel 3:2</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://bible.oremus.org/?passage=Joel%203:12&amp;version=nrsv">3:12</a>): "I will gather together all nations, and will bring them down into the valley of Josaphat: "Then I will enter into judgment with them there", on behalf of my people and for My inheritance Israel, whom they have scattered among the nations and they have divided up My land."; "Let the nations be roused; Let the nations be aroused And come up to the Valley of Jehoshaphat, for there I will sit to judge all the nations on every side". This location is also referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Valley_of_Decision" title="Valley of Decision">Valley of Decision</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Identification">Identification</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_of_Josaphat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Identification"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="In_the_Judaean_Desert_near_Teqo.27a"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="In_the_Judaean_Desert_near_Teqo'a">In the Judaean Desert near Teqo'a</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_of_Josaphat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In the Judaean Desert near Teqo&#039;a"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By one interpretation, this describes the place where, in the presence of <a href="/wiki/Jehoshaphat" title="Jehoshaphat">Jehoshaphat</a> (Josaphat), <a href="/wiki/King_of_Judah" class="mw-redirect" title="King of Judah">King of Judah</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yahweh" title="Yahweh">Yahweh</a> annihilated the <a href="/wiki/Gentile" title="Gentile">Gentile</a> coalition of <a href="/wiki/Moab" title="Moab">Moab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ammon" title="Ammon">Ammon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edom" title="Edom">Edom</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This may have indicated an actual valley euphemistically called by the <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> <i>êmêq Berâkâh</i> ("valley of blessing"), situated in the <a href="/wiki/Judaean_Desert" title="Judaean Desert">Judaean Desert</a>, in the proximity of <a href="/wiki/Teqoa" title="Teqoa">Teqo'a</a> near <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khirbet_Ber%C3%AAk%C3%BBt&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Khirbet Berêkût (page does not exist)">Khirbet Berêkût</a>, west of <a href="/wiki/Teqoa" title="Teqoa">Khirbet Teqû'a</a> (about eleven miles from <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a>).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Upper_Kidron_Valley_segment">Upper Kidron Valley segment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_of_Josaphat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Upper Kidron Valley segment"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the fourth century, in the <a href="/wiki/Itinerarium_Burdigalense" title="Itinerarium Burdigalense">Bordeaux itinerary</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Kidron_Valley" title="Kidron Valley">Cedron</a> takes the name of Valley of Josaphat. <a href="/wiki/Eusebius" title="Eusebius">Eusebius</a> (in his <i><a href="/wiki/Onomasticon_(Eusebius)" title="Onomasticon (Eusebius)">Onomasticon</a></i>) and <a href="/wiki/St._Jerome" class="mw-redirect" title="St. Jerome">St. Jerome</a> strengthen this view, while <a href="/wiki/Cyril_of_Alexandria" title="Cyril of Alexandria">Cyril of Alexandria</a> appears to indicate a different place; early Jewish tradition denied the reality of this valley. Subsequently to the fourth century, <a href="/wiki/Christians" title="Christians">Christians</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">Jews</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muslims" title="Muslims">Muslims</a> regard Cedron as the place of the <a href="/wiki/Last_Judgment" title="Last Judgment">Last Judgment</a>. What has lent colour to this popular belief is the fact that since the time of the kings of Judah, Cedron has been the principal <a href="/wiki/Necropolis" title="Necropolis">necropolis</a> of Jerusalem. <a href="/wiki/Josias" class="mw-redirect" title="Josias">Josias</a> scattered upon the tombs of the children of Israel the ashes of the idol of the goddess <a href="/wiki/Astarte" title="Astarte">Astarte</a> which he burned in Cedron (<a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://bible.oremus.org/?passage=2%20Kings%2023:4&amp;version=nrsv">2 Kings 23:4</a>).
</p><p>The designation of a Valley of Jehoshaphat is applied as a specific <a href="/wiki/Toponym" class="mw-redirect" title="Toponym">toponym</a> for the first time by the <a href="/wiki/Pilgrim_of_Bordeaux" class="mw-redirect" title="Pilgrim of Bordeaux">Pilgrim of Bordeaux</a> in 333.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Since then it has become a general designation for the <a href="/wiki/Kidron_Valley" title="Kidron Valley">Kidron Valley</a>, between Jerusalem and the <a href="/wiki/Mount_of_Olives" title="Mount of Olives">Mount of Olives</a>, which is repeatedly mentioned in the Old and New Testaments under its own name.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Symbolic.2C_indeterminate_place"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Symbolic,_indeterminate_place">Symbolic, indeterminate place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_of_Josaphat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Symbolic, indeterminate place"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alternatively, it may refer to an indeterminate valley of judgment, since "Josaphat" means "Yahweh judges". In <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://bible.oremus.org/?passage=Joel%203:14&amp;version=nrsv">Joel 3:14</a> the same valley is called the "<a href="/wiki/Valley_of_Decision" title="Valley of Decision">valley of decision</a>" (or in the <a href="/wiki/Douay%E2%80%93Rheims_Bible" title="Douay–Rheims Bible">Douay–Rheims Bible</a> "valley of destruction"<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>). The chapter in question describes how the nations that afflicted Judah and Jerusalem during their <a href="/wiki/Babylonian_captivity" title="Babylonian captivity">Babylonian captivity</a> and return from exile shall receive <a href="/wiki/Divine_judgment" title="Divine judgment">Divine judgment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="No_place">No place</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Valley_of_Josaphat&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: No place"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/Midrash_Tehillim" title="Midrash Tehillim">Midrash Tehillim</a>, no "valley called Jehoshaphat" exists.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
