         <h1 id="community">Community</h1>
<p>Haskellers interact, talk and collaborate across several mediums and around the world. There are places to learn, to teach, to ask questions, and to find contributors and collaborators.</p>
<p><a href="../donations/">Help support the community!</a></p>
<h2 id="online-communities-and-social-resources">Online Communities and Social Resources</h2>
<p>Haskellers are active on a number of online areas, but the most busy are below:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="../mailing-lists/">The Haskell mailing lists</a></li>
<li><a href="../irc/">IRC (online chat)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://matrix.to/#/#haskell:matrix.org">Matrix (online chat)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://discourse.haskell.org">Haskell Community Discourse</a></li>
<li><a href="http://stackoverflow.com/questions/tagged?tagnames=haskell">StackOverflow</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/programming.haskell/">Facebook community</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.reddit.com/r/haskell">Reddit</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org">Wiki</a></li>
<li><a href="http://planet.haskell.org/">The blogosphere</a></li>
<li><a href="https://haskellweekly.news/">Haskell Weekly e-mail newsletter</a></li>
<li><a href="https://gitter.im/haskell-chat">Haskell Gitter Community Chat</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/Haskell_Communities_and_Activities_Report">Haskell Communities and Activities Report</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 id="in-person-groups-and-meetups">In-person Groups and Meetups</h2>
<p>There are a number of Haskell Users groups where haskellers meet to learn and code. Some are listed below:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/ATX-Haskell/">Austin Haskell Users Group</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/Bay-Area-Haskell-Users-Group/">Bay Area Haskell Users Group</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/Boston-Haskell/">Boston Haskell</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/berlinhug/">Berlin Haskell Users Group</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/Brisbane-Functional-Programming-Group/">Brisbane Functional Programming Group</a></li>
<li><a href="http://ChicagoHaskell.com/">Chicago Haskell</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.meetup.com/haskell-dublin-meetup/">Dublin Haskell Meetup</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.meetup.com/Haskell-DC/">Haskell DC</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.haskell-ita.it/">Italy Haskell Users Group</a></li>
<li><a href="http://haskell.jp/">Japan Haskell Users Group (Haskell-jp)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/NY-Haskell/">New York Haskell Users Group</a></li>
<li><a href="https://muenchen.haskell.bayern/">Munich Haskell Meeting</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/London-Haskell/">London Haskell</a></li>
<li><a href="http://seattlehaskell.org/">Seattle Area Haskell Users’ Group (SeaHUG)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.meetup.com/find/?allMeetups=true&amp;keywords=Haskell&amp;radius=Infinity">More Haskell meetups at meetup.com</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 id="committees">Committees</h2>
<p>Haskell infrastructure is mostly maintained by committees and groups of volunteers. Here are some of the committees involved in keeping things running:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/Core_Libraries_Committee">Core Libraries Committee</a></li>
<li><a href="https://github.com/ghc-proposals/ghc-proposals">GHC Steering Committee</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/Hackage_trustees">Hackage trustees</a></li>
<li><a href="../haskell-org-committee">Haskell.org committee</a></li>
<li><a href="https://docs.haskellstack.org/en/stable/maintainers/team_process/">Stackage maintainers</a></li>
<li><a href="https://github.com/haskell-infra/haskell-admins/">Haskell admin team</a>: responsible for all <code>***.haskell.org</code> infrastructure, including this website.</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="conferences-and-events">Conferences and Events</h2>
<p>There are a number of conferences and events featuring Haskell, some focusing on the academic side of things, and some on the commercial or hobbyist side. Here are just a few:</p>
<h3 id="academic-conferences">Academic Conferences</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.haskell.org/haskell-symposium/">The Haskell Symposium</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/HaskellImplementorsWorkshop">Haskell Implementors’ Workshop</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.icfpconference.org/">The International Conference on Functional Programming</a></li>
<li><a href="https://www.sigplan.org/Conferences/POPL/">Symposium on Principles of Programming Languages</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.ifl-symposia.org/">International Symposia on Implementation and Application of Functional Languages</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.tifp.org/">Symposium on Trends in Functional Programming</a></li>
</ul>
<h3 id="non-academic-conferences">Non-Academic Conferences</h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://cufp.org/">Commercial Users of Functional Programming (Roving)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://lambdajam.yowconference.com.au/">YOW! LambdaJam (Sydney, AU)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://functionalconf.com/">Functional Conf (Bangalore, IN)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://bobkonf.de/">BOB (Berlin, DE)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://skillsmatter.com/conferences/11741-haskell-exchange-2019">Skills Matter’s Haskell eXchange (London, GB)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://lambdaconf.us/">LambdaConf (Boulder, CO, USA)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.lambda.world/">Lambda World (Cádiz, Spain and Seattle, USA)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://composeconference.org">Compose :: Conference (NY, NY, USA)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://composeconference.org">Compose :: Melbourne (Melbourne, VIC, AU)</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.curry-on.org/">Curry On (Europe, roving)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://lambdale.org/">LambdAle (London, GB)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://haskell.love">Haskell Love Conference (online)</a></li>
</ul>
<h3 id="hackathons">Hackathons</h3>
<p>Haskell Hackathons are a long tradition, with lots of learning and social exchange. In many ways they function as semi-structured conferences. Here are some of the most notable:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/BayHac">BayHac (San Francisco Bay Area, CA, USA)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/Hac_Phi">Hac Phi (Philadelphia, PA, USA)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/ZuriHac">ZuriHac (Zurich, CH)</a></li>
<li><a href="https://wiki.haskell.org/HacFreiburg2017">HacFreiburg (Freiburg, De)</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 id="specific-interest-groups">Specific Interest Groups</h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="http://industry.haskell.org/">Industrial Haskell Group</a></li>
<li><a href="http://commercialhaskell.com/">Commercial Haskell Group</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.datahaskell.org/">DataHaskell: data science, machine learning, numerical computation and related</a></li>
<li><a href="http://lurk.org/groups/haskell-art/">Haskell Art</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 id="supporting-the-community">Supporting the Community</h2>
<p>If you would like to support the Haskell community, consider <a href="../donations">donating to Haskell.org</a>, the organization that maintains a significant part of the Haskell community infrastructure.</p>
