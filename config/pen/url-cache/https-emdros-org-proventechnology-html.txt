<h2>Emdros is a proven technology</h2>

<h3>Commercial use</h3>

<P>I have <strong>sold an Emdros license</strong> to <A
HREF="http://www.logos.com">Logos Bible Software</A>.  Logos is the
world's leading vendor of Bible Software, and Emdros powers the Syntax
Search in their Libronix product, as well as a "grammatical sketch",
similar to Adam Kilgariff's <A
HREF="http://www.sketchengine.co.uk">Sketch Engine</A>, but for the
Biblical original languages.</P>

<P>I have <strong>sold an Emdros license</strong> to the <A
HREF="http://www.dbg.de/">German Bible Society</A> for use in their <A
HREF="http://www.logos.com/products/details/1981">SESB product</A>
(Stuttgart Electronic Study Bible).  SESB will use Emdros as a basis
for searching a Hebrew database.</P>

<h3>Academic use</h3>

<P>Emdros is being used with great success in a number of places,
mostly academic settings with linguistic database needs.</P>

<P>Among them is a large-scale Hebrew database project at the <A
HREF="http://www.th.vu.nl/~wiweb/" target="_blank">Vrije Universiteit
Amsterdam</A>.  We have imported a 420,000-word corpus of syntactic
analyses with 1.4 million syntactic objects, and Emdros has proven to
be fast and reliable.</P>

<P>Linguists at one of the Universities in Toulouse are using Emdros
in their research.  Emdros powers the "Keskya" concordancer, developed
at <A HREF="http://www.irit.fr">IRIT</A>, Toulouse, France, which
helps the linguists conduct their corpus-based research.</P>

<P>The Thai Treebank used to have an online, searcheable interface
powered by Emdros.  It appears to be offline for now, however.</P>

<h3>Government use</h3>

<P>The <A HREF="http://www.knaw.nl">Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts
and Sciences</A> (KNAW) have a subdivision called "<A
HREF="http://www.dans.knaw.nl/en">DANS</A>", which stands for "Data
Archiving and Networked Services".  The DANS have selected Emdros to
be the underlying database technology for at least two projects aimed
at making the Dutch computational linguistic research legacy available
and archivable online.  One of them can be seen <A
HREF="http://wivu.dans.knaw.nl">here</A>.</P>
