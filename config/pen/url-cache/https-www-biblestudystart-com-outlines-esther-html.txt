 </tr>

  <td class="verses">Es 1:10-12.</td>
  <td class="summary">Vashti, sent for, refuses to come.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 1:13-22.</td>
  <td class="summary">Ahasuerus, by the counsel of Memucan, puts away Vashti, and makes the decree of men's sovereignty.</td>
 </tr>

 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 2:1-4.</td>
  <td class="summary">Out of the choice of virgins a queen is to be chosen.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 2:5-7.</td>
  <td class="summary">Mordecai the nursing father of Esther.</td>
 </tr>

  <td class="summary">Esther preferred before the rest.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 2:12-14.</td>
  <td class="summary">The manner of purification, and going in to the king.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 2:15-20.</td>
  <td class="summary">Esther best pleasing the king, is made queen.</td>
 </tr>

  <td class="verses">Es 2:21-23.</td>
  <td class="summary">Mordecai discovering a treason, is recorded in the chronicles.</td>
 </tr>

 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 3:1-6.</td>
  <td class="summary">Haman, advanced by the king, and despised by Mordecai, seeks revenge upon all the Jews.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 3:7.</td>
  <td class="summary">He casts lots.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 3:8-15.</td>
  <td class="summary">He obtains by calumniation a decree of the king to put the Jews to death.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td colspan="2" class="chapterheading">Esther chapter 4</td></tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 4:1-3.</td>
  <td class="summary">The great mourning of Mordecai and the Jews.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 4:4-9.</td>
  <td class="summary">Esther, understanding it, sends to Mordecai, who shews the cause, and advises her to undertake the suit.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 4:10-14.</td>
  <td class="summary">She excusing herself, is threatened by Mordecai.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 4:15-17.</td>
  <td class="summary">She appointing a fast, undertakes the suit.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td colspan="2" class="chapterheading">Esther chapter 5</td></tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 5:1-5.</td>
  <td class="summary">Esther, adventuring on the king's favour, obtains the grace of the golden sceptre, and invites the king and Haman to a banquet.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 5:6-8.</td>
  <td class="summary">She, being encouraged by the king in her suit, invites them to another banquet the next day.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 5:9-13.</td>
  <td class="summary">Haman, proud of his advancement, repines at the contempt of Mordecai.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 5:14.</td>
  <td class="summary">By the counsel of Zeresh he prepares for him a gallows.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td colspan="2" class="chapterheading">Esther chapter 6</td></tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 6:1-3.</td>
  <td class="summary">Ahasuerus, reading in the chronicles of the good service done by Mordecai, takes care for his reward.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 6:4-11.</td>
  <td class="summary">Haman, coming to sue that Mordecai might be hanged, gives counsel that he might do him honour.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 6:12-14.</td>
  <td class="summary">Complaining of this, his friends tell him of his final destiny.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td colspan="2" class="chapterheading">Esther chapter 7</td></tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 7:1-4.</td>
  <td class="summary">Esther, entertaining the king and Haman, makes suit for her own life, and her people's.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 7:5-6.</td>
  <td class="summary">She accuses Haman.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 7:7-10.</td>
  <td class="summary">The king in his anger, understanding of the gallows which Haman had made for Mordecai, causes him to be hanged thereon.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td colspan="2" class="chapterheading">Esther chapter 8</td></tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 8:1-2.</td>
  <td class="summary">Mordecai is advanced.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 8:3-6.</td>
  <td class="summary">Esther makes suit to reverse Haman's letters.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 8:7-14.</td>
  <td class="summary">Ahasuerus grants to the Jews to defend themselves.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 8:15-17.</td>
  <td class="summary">Mordecai's honour, and the Jews' joy.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td colspan="2" class="chapterheading">Esther chapter 9</td></tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 9:1-11.</td>
  <td class="summary">The Jews slay their enemies, with the ten sons of Haman.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 9:12-19.</td>
  <td class="summary">Ahasuerus, at the request of Esther, grants another day of slaughter, and Haman's sons to be hanged.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 9:20-32.</td>
  <td class="summary">The two days of Purim are made festival.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr><td colspan="2" class="chapterheading">Esther chapter 10</td></tr>
 <tr>
  <td class="verses">Es 10:1-2.</td>
  <td class="summary">Ahasuerus' greatness.</td>
 </tr>
 <tr>
