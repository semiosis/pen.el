     <h2>Training at the Botanic Garden</h2><p>We have an international reputation for quality training and education programmes. We enjoy teaching and have the experience and resources to provide you with practical learning experiences to assist you in furthering your knowledge and skills.</p><h2>Current opportunities</h2><h3>Apprentice training</h3><p>This is a three-year contract of work and study, which, if completed successfully, will result in qualifying with the NZ Certificate in Amenity Horticulture (Level 4). This qualification is highly respected within the horticulture industry in NZ and overseas.</p><p>It involves working five days a week in the Botanic Garden and your own time theoretical study, supported by the Horticultural Training Organisation. You will be asked to keep a daily work diary, sit monthly plant identification tests and attend annual block courses.</p><p>For more details, please contact 4Trades Apprenticeships, Business South, Apprentice Facilitator - Botanic Garden, phone 479 0181.</p><h3>Project Green jobs</h3><p>We provide work opportunities to qualifying students of Southern Youth Development. This small team works in the Botanic Garden learning important workplace skills while gaining practical experience in grounds maintenance. Contact Southern Youth Development on 477 4175 or visit their website at <a href="https://syd.org.nz/programmes/gardens-at-syd/" title="Southern Youth Development" target="_blank">www.syd.org.nz/</a>.</p><h3>Otago Polytechnic Horticulture</h3><p>We have a long standing relationship with the Otago Polytechnic where we provide the opportunity for horticultural students to complete the practical component of their NZ National Certificate in Horticulture here at the Botanic Garden.</p><p>For more information, or to enrol for one of their courses, contact the Otago Polytechnic Natural Resources Department.</p><h3>International student volunteers</h3><p>Horticultural/botanical work experience placements are from one to 12 months long, giving students an opportunity to expand and challenge their knowledge and skills.</p><h3>International academic training placements</h3><p>Available for overseas students studying at horticultural institutions and colleges who are required to train offshore to meet course objectives.</p><h3>Work placement</h3><p>Available for those who have completed training in a related field of horticulture or botany who wish to further their experience and training.</p><h3>Work experience</h3><p>We offer work experience to tertiary school students who are seriously thinking about horticulture as a career.</p><!--

<h3>Work Placement</h3>
<p><strong>Applications in this area are not being accepted for now.</strong></p>
<p>For people no longer in horticultural training who need to further their experience and training. To find out more contact Barbara Wheeler, Collections Supervisor, Dunedin Botanic Garden 477 4000 or <a href="mailto:botanic@dcc.govt.nz">botanic@dcc.govt.nz</a></p>
<h3>Apprentices</h3>
<p>After a 3-year contract of work and study, this apprenticeship will result in the NZ Certificate in Amenity Horticulture (Level 4). The qualification is highly respected by the horticulture industry both in NZ and overseas.</p>
<p>You will work a 5-day week in the Botanic Garden plus carry out theoretical study with Otago Polytechnic. You need to keep a daily work diary, sit monthly plant identification tests and attend annual block courses of 2 weeks with Otago Polytechnic.</p>
<p>Apprentices are paid industry rates and have normal work entitlements such as leave. Every 6 months you will be rotated to a new plant collection to develop an understanding of all aspects of plant collection management, development and maintenance.</p>
<p>4Trades Apprenticeships, Otago Chamber of Commerce, Apprentice Facilitator - Botanic Garden, phone 479 0181.</p>
<h3>Work Experience</h3>
<p><strong>Applications in this area are not being accepted for now.</strong></p>
<p>We offer work experience to school students who are serious about horticulture and want to see if it is a career option for them. Ask your school to arrange a week or two's work experience with us.</p>
<p>To find out more contact Barbara Wheeler, Collections Supervisor, Dunedin Botanic Garden 477 4000 or <a href="mailto:botanic@dcc.govt.nz">botanic@dcc.govt.nz</a></p>
<h3>Project Green Jobs</h3>
<p>We provide 6 month practical experience/work for the full time Malcam Trust Project Green Jobs programme.</p>
<p>This small team works in the Botanic Garden learning workplace requirements and vital practical experience in Grounds Maintenance. Contact the Malcam Trust on 477 4175 or visit their website at <a href="https://malcam.org.nz/" _blank="" title="Malcam">www.malcam.org.nz</a>.</p>
<h2>Otago Polytechnic Horticulture Courses</h2>
<p>We provide the practical component for the New Zealand National Certificate in Horticulture course, in partnership with the Otago Polytechnic.</p>
<p>For more information or to enrol, contact the Otago Polytechnic Natural Resources Department.</p>-->
