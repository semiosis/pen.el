<H2>
Ignatius of Antioch
<FONT SIZE=-1>
(born ? -- died (apparently martyred) ~110 CE in Rome) &#160;&#160;
</FONT>
</H2>

<table  width=250 align=right  border=2 cellpadding=2>

<P>
Ignatius, bishop of Antioch in Syria, is known mainly as the author of 7
letters that had exceptional influence in the early church.
A Catholic Encyclopedia article is online at
<A HREF="http://www.newadvent.org/cathen/07644a.htm">St. Ignatius of Antioch</A>.

He was apparently anxious to counteract the teachings of two groups:
the Judaizers, who did not accept the authority of the New Testament
(although the NT did not really exist at that time); and the Docetists,
who held that Christ's sufferings and death were only apparent.
The letters have often been cited to determine what beliefs were held in the early church.
These letters are:

<P>Ignatius does not refer to older Christian writings by name,
but his letters have quotations (of approval) from these writings:
<UL>
<LI><A HREF="#Gospel_of_Matthew">Gospel according to Matthew</A></LI>
<LI><A HREF="#Gospel_of_Luke">Gospel according to Luke</A></LI>
<LI><A HREF="#Acts">Acts</A></LI>
<LI><A HREF="#Romans">Romans</A></LI>
<LI><A HREF="#Corinthians">I Corinthians</A></LI>
<LI><A HREF="#Ephesians">Ephesians</A></LI>
<LI><A HREF="#Colossians">Colossians</A></LI>
<LI><A HREF="#Thessalonians">I Thessalonians</A></LI>
</UL>


<P>
Ignatius is held in high regard in the West Syrian Church (an independent Christian Church).
Every patriarch since 1293 bears the surname "Ignatios" in his honor.
The present (1982) patriarch, Ignatios XXXIX Jacob III, rules from Damascus over
11 metropolitans and 3 bishops and over the Syrian Orthodox Church in Malabar.
