                <h1 class="page-hero__title">Grade score marking</h1>
            
            <p class="page-hero__summary">How we use grade score marking for external assessments</p>
        </div>
    </div>

                    <p>We use grade score marking for all achievement standards.</p><p>All external achievement standards have a single outcome. Markers can measure a candidate's performance by gathering assessment evidence from all parts of a paper.</p><h3>A well-researched approach to marking</h3><p>We did 5 years of research before bringing in this way of marking.</p><p>That research confirmed that grade score marking is a fair and effective method of standards-based marking.</p>

                        <h2 class="block__title content-element__title">How we mark questions</h2>
                    
                    <p>We award a single grade for each question in an NCEA paper:</p><ul>
<li>Not Achieved (N)</li>
<li>Achieved (A)</li>
<li>Merit (M)</li>
<li>Excellence (E).</li>
</ul><h3>We use evidence from the assessment response</h3><p>We use the criteria from the standard to award this grade. We base the grade on the quality of evidence in the response.</p><p>Markers are instructed to ensure a high-quality response is not marked down for a minor error.</p><h3>We look for evidence of high performance first</h3><p>Marking is<span>&nbsp;'</span>top down'.</p><p>Markers must first&nbsp;look for evidence for Excellence, as described by the criterion for Excellence in the standard.</p><p>If they don't find this evidence, markers then look for Merit evidence, and then down to Achievement.&nbsp;</p><h3>Grades are based on the candidate's whole response</h3><p>The grade is based on the whole response to the question. It takes account of all evidence in the candidate’s answer.</p><p>Some questions may have parts or bullet points, but this does not stop markers giving a single holistic grade.</p><h3>We identify if the assessment response is upper or lower within a grade</h3><p>Grade score marking also recognises the quality of evidence within each grade. Grades can show an upper and lower result in a grade.</p><p>For example, lower Merit is M5 and upper Merit is M6. Both 5 and 6 are Merit scores.</p><p>The scores indicate that the student has met the criterion for a Merit grade in the question.</p><h4>Possible grade scores for a<span>&nbsp;</span>question</h4><table>

                        <h2 class="block__title content-element__title">Calculating the overall grade for a standard</h2>
                    
                    <p>We come to a single grade for overall performance against the standard by combining the scores from the questions.</p><p>For example, in an assessment paper with 3 questions, a candidate scores M5 for Question 1, A3 for Question 2, and M6 for Question 3. For the whole paper, the candidate’s score is M5 + A3 + M6 = 14.</p><p>This score will be written in the 'total' box on the front cover of the candidate’s answer booklet.</p><h3>We check the total score against the cut scores for the standard</h3><p>During marking in each year, the senior markers set the<span>&nbsp;</span>cut scores. These are the scores where the different grades separate.</p><p>The senior markers use the standard and a large sample of papers on each score to decide these cut scores.</p><p>It is essential to the integrity of grade score marking that the cut scores are based on a holistic judgement across actual papers.</p><p>The cut scores may be written as judgement statements: either as a range of numbers (for example, Merit range 14 to 19) or as a single number (for example, Merit 14).</p><p>In this example:</p><ul>
<li>any paper&nbsp;with a score of 14 written in the 'total' box would receive a Merit grade</li>
<li>any paper with a score of 19 written in the 'total' box would receive a Merit grade</li>
<li>all papers between and including 14 to 19 receive a Merit grade.</li>
</ul><p>Each set of cut scores relates only to that particular assessment.</p><h4>There are new cut scores each year</h4><p>Each year, we set new grade score ranges using marked papers and the standard being assessed.</p><p>We publish these cut scores at the same time as papers are returned to candidates. This allows candidates to check whether their grade as shown in their online results is correct.</p><p><a href="/ncea/subjects/cut-scores/">Cut scores</a></p>
                </div>
            </div>

            
        </div>
    </div>
