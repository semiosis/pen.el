        <h2>A hymn on the life, death and resurrection of Christ</h2>
        <div class="flex-container">
			                <div class="video">
                    <div class="embed-container"><iframe title="Story Behind The Song In Christ Alone Stuart Townend" width="640" height="360" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/TpipuCeGQn4?feature=oembed" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" allowfullscreen></iframe></div>
                </div>             <div class="story-copy-video"><p>In Christ alone was actually the first song Keith Getty and I wrote together. We had been introduced by a mutual friend at a worship conference, where we had a coffee together and talked about our backgrounds, musical influences, and motivations to write. Keith promised he would send me a CD with a bunch of melodies he&#8217;d been working on, and to be honest I thought no more about it.</p>
<p>Then a few days later the CD arrived. And the first melody on it really hit me &#8211; it was tuneful and memorable, and yet had gravitas and real emotion. And I began to feel the pressure to write lyrics that were of a comparable standard!</p>
<p>The only thing I could think was to base it on the eternal theme of the life, death and resurrection of Christ. The verses came in a fairly linear way, but as the third verse developed, I was getting pretty excited as I thought about the amazing implications of Christ&#8217;s finished work on the cross. I wanted to write a fourth verse that was about us &#8211; but not just as an emotional response, but as a undeniable statement of the power of Christ to sustain us in this life.</p>
<p>Keith and I are overwhelmed with the response this song has had, and we are just grateful to God that He should use it to build up His church in this way.</p>
</div>
