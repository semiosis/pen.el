<p><b>John Carson Lennox</b> (born 7 November 1943) is an English <a href="/wiki/Mathematician" title="Mathematician">mathematician</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bioethicist" class="mw-redirect" title="Bioethicist">bioethicist</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Christian_apologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Christian apologist">Christian apologist</a> originally from Northern Ireland. He has written many books on religion, ethics, the relationship between science and God (like his books, <i>Has Science Buried God</i> and <i>Can Science Explain Everything</i>), and has had public debates with atheists including <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dawkins" title="Richard Dawkins">Richard Dawkins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hitchens" title="Christopher Hitchens">Christopher Hitchens</a>.
</p><p>Lennox earned his first doctorate from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a>, then earned a second and third doctorate from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_University" title="Cardiff University">Cardiff University</a>, respectively. As a professor, Lennox specialised in <a href="/wiki/Group_theory" title="Group theory">group theory</a>. He is Emeritus Professor of <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">Mathematics</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>, where he is also Emeritus Fellow in Mathematics and <a href="/wiki/Philosophy_of_Science" class="mw-redirect" title="Philosophy of Science">Philosophy of Science</a> at <a href="/wiki/Green_Templeton_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Green Templeton College">Green Templeton College</a> and has worked as <a href="/wiki/Adjunct_lecturer" class="mw-redirect" title="Adjunct lecturer">adjunct lecturer</a> at <a href="/wiki/Wycliffe_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Wycliffe Hall">Wycliffe Hall</a> and at the Oxford Centre for Christian Apologetics. He is also an Associate Fellow of the <a href="/wiki/Sa%C3%AFd_Business_School" title="Saïd Business School">Saïd Business School</a> and a Senior Fellow at the <a href="/wiki/Trinity_Forum" title="Trinity Forum">Trinity Forum</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lennox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>John Lennox was born on 7 November 1943 in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> and brought up in <a href="/wiki/Armagh" title="Armagh">Armagh</a> where his father ran a store.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/The_Royal_School,_Armagh" title="The Royal School, Armagh">The Royal School, Armagh</a>, and went on to become Exhibitioner and Senior Scholar at <a href="/wiki/Emmanuel_College,_Cambridge" title="Emmanuel College, Cambridge">Emmanuel College, Cambridge</a>, where in 1962 he also attended the last lectures of <a href="/wiki/C._S._Lewis" title="C. S. Lewis">C.&#160;S. Lewis</a> on the poet <a href="/wiki/John_Donne" title="John Donne">John Donne</a>. Lennox obtained a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts_(Oxbridge_and_Dublin)" class="mw-redirect" title="Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)">Master of Arts</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Mathematics" title="Master of Mathematics">Master of Mathematics</a> and a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">University of Cambridge</a> with the dissertation <i>Centrality and Permutability in Soluble Groups</i> (1970).<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Lennox was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Science" title="Doctor of Science">Doctor of Science</a> degree in <a href="/wiki/Mathematics" title="Mathematics">mathematics</a> by <a href="/wiki/Cardiff_University" title="Cardiff University">Cardiff University</a> for his research. Lennox also holds a <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Philosophy" title="Doctor of Philosophy">Doctor of Philosophy</a> degree from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a> (by incorporation)<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> and an <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Arts" title="Master of Arts">M.A.</a> degree in <a href="/wiki/Bioethics" title="Bioethics">bioethics</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Surrey" title="University of Surrey">University of Surrey</a>. In addition, he was a <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Fellow" class="mw-redirect" title="Humboldt Fellow">Humboldt Fellow</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_W%C3%BCrzburg" title="University of Würzburg">University of Würzburg</a> and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Freiburg" title="University of Freiburg">University of Freiburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lennox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Upon completing his doctorate, Lennox moved to <a href="/wiki/Cardiff" title="Cardiff">Cardiff</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, becoming a <a href="/wiki/Reader_(academic_rank)" title="Reader (academic rank)">reader</a> in Mathematics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wales" title="University of Wales">University of Wales</a>, Cardiff. Lennox teaches <a href="/wiki/Science_and_religion" class="mw-redirect" title="Science and religion">science and religion</a> in the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>. During his 29 years in Cardiff he spent a year at each of the universities of <a href="/wiki/W%C3%BCrzburg" title="Würzburg">Würzburg</a>, <a href="/wiki/Freiburg" class="mw-redirect" title="Freiburg">Freiburg</a> (as an <a href="/wiki/Alexander_von_Humboldt_Fellow" class="mw-redirect" title="Alexander von Humboldt Fellow">Alexander von Humboldt Fellow</a>), and <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, and has lectured extensively in both Eastern and Western Europe, <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> on mathematics, <a href="/wiki/Apologetics" title="Apologetics">apologetics</a>, and the exposition of <a href="/wiki/Scripture" class="mw-redirect" title="Scripture">scripture</a>.
</p><p>Lennox is the author of a number of books on the relations of science, religion, and ethics. He has published over 70 peer-reviewed articles on mathematics, co-authored two Oxford Mathematical Monographs, and worked as a translator of Russian mathematics.
</p><p>He has spoken in many different countries, in conferences and as an academic fellow, including numerous trips to the former <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 14 March 2012 he presented an edition of the <a href="/wiki/Lent_Talks" title="Lent Talks">Lent Talks</a> for <a href="/wiki/BBC_Radio_Four" class="mw-redirect" title="BBC Radio Four">BBC Radio Four</a>. Lennox has also given lectures at the <a href="/wiki/Veritas_Forum" title="Veritas Forum">Veritas Forum</a> on topics such as the relationship between science and religion,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Existence_of_god" class="mw-redirect" title="Existence of god">existence of God</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Doubt" title="Doubt">doubt</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Problem_of_evil" title="Problem of evil">problems of evil and suffering</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Additionally, he is a Senior Fellow of The Trinity Forum, a Christian nonprofit organisation that develops leaders to make contributions to cultural renewal.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Debates">Debates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lennox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Debates"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:John_Lennox_and_Christopher_Hitchens_debating.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/John_Lennox_and_Christopher_Hitchens_debating.jpg/220px-John_Lennox_and_Christopher_Hitchens_debating.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="112" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/John_Lennox_and_Christopher_Hitchens_debating.jpg/330px-John_Lennox_and_Christopher_Hitchens_debating.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/John_Lennox_and_Christopher_Hitchens_debating.jpg/440px-John_Lennox_and_Christopher_Hitchens_debating.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3153" data-file-height="1602" /></a><figcaption>Lennox (left) debating religion with <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hitchens" title="Christopher Hitchens">Christopher Hitchens</a> in Alabama, March 2009</figcaption></figure>
<p>Lennox has been part of numerous public debates defending the Christian faith, including debates with <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hitchens" title="Christopher Hitchens">Christopher Hitchens</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Shermer" title="Michael Shermer">Michael Shermer</a>, <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dawkins" title="Richard Dawkins">Richard Dawkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lawrence_Krauss" title="Lawrence Krauss">Lawrence Krauss</a>,
<a href="/wiki/Peter_Atkins" title="Peter Atkins">Peter Atkins</a>, <a href="/wiki/Victor_Stenger" class="mw-redirect" title="Victor Stenger">Victor Stenger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Michael_Tooley" title="Michael Tooley">Michael Tooley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Law" title="Stephen Law">Stephen Law</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Peter_Singer" title="Peter Singer">Peter Singer</a>.
</p>
<ul><li>On 3 October 2007, Lennox debated <a href="/wiki/Richard_Dawkins" title="Richard Dawkins">Richard Dawkins</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Alabama_at_Birmingham" title="University of Alabama at Birmingham">University of Alabama at Birmingham</a> in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama" title="Birmingham, Alabama">Birmingham, Alabama</a> on Dawkins's views expressed in his book, <i><a href="/wiki/The_God_Delusion" title="The God Delusion">The God Delusion</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-debate_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-debate-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Lennox and Dawkins had a discussion in April 2008 at <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Oxford" title="Trinity College, Oxford">Trinity College, Oxford</a> to expand upon topics left undeveloped during The God Delusion Debate.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 9 August 2008, Lennox debated <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Hitchens" title="Christopher Hitchens">Christopher Hitchens</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh_International_Festival" title="Edinburgh International Festival">Edinburgh International Festival</a> in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Edinburgh, Scotland">Edinburgh, Scotland</a>, on the question of whether or not Europe should jettison its religious past and welcome the "<a href="/wiki/New_Atheism" title="New Atheism">New Atheism</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 23 August 2008, Lennox debated <a href="/wiki/Michael_Shermer" title="Michael Shermer">Michael Shermer</a> at the Wesley Conference Centre in <a href="/wiki/Sydney,_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Sydney, Australia">Sydney, Australia</a>, on the existence of God.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 21 October 2008, Lennox again debated Dawkins at the <a href="/wiki/Oxford_University_Museum_of_Natural_History" title="Oxford University Museum of Natural History">Oxford University Museum of Natural History</a>, the site of the <a href="/wiki/1860_Oxford_evolution_debate" title="1860 Oxford evolution debate">1860 Oxford evolution debate</a> between <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Henry_Huxley" title="Thomas Henry Huxley">Thomas Henry Huxley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Wilberforce" title="Samuel Wilberforce">Samuel Wilberforce</a>. The debate was titled "Has Science Buried God?".<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Spectator" title="The Spectator">The Spectator</a></i> called the event "Huxley-Wilberforce, Round Two."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 3 March 2009, Lennox debated Hitchens for the second time at <a href="/wiki/Samford_University" title="Samford University">Samford University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Birmingham,_Alabama" title="Birmingham, Alabama">Birmingham, Alabama</a>, on the question "Is God Great?" The debate addressed the validity of some of Hitchens' claims in his book <i><a href="/wiki/God_is_Not_Great" class="mw-redirect" title="God is Not Great">God is Not Great</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 20 July 2011, Lennox debated <a href="/wiki/Peter_Singer" title="Peter Singer">Peter Singer</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Melbourne_Town_Hall" title="Melbourne Town Hall">Melbourne Town Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Australia" class="mw-redirect" title="Melbourne, Australia">Melbourne, Australia</a> on the topic "Is There a God?"<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In August 2021, Lennox was the chief guest for the prestigious Malhar Fest hosted by St. Xavier's College, Mumbai.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=John_Lennox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Personal life"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Lennox speaks English, Russian, French, Spanish and German. He is married to Sally and has three children and 10 grandchildren.<sup id="cite_ref-debate_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-debate-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> He has a brother named Gilbert Lennox, previously an elder in Glenabbey Church, <a href="/wiki/Glengormley" title="Glengormley">Glengormley</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> The hymn writer and recording artist <a href="/wiki/Kristyn_Getty" title="Kristyn Getty">Kristyn Getty</a> is John's niece, being Gilbert's daughter.
</p>
