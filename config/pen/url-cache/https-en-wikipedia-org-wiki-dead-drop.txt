	<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading mw-first-heading">Dead drop</h1>
	<div id="bodyContent" class="vector-body">
		<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>
		<div id="contentSub"></div>
		<div id="contentSub2"></div>
		
		<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
		<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#searchInput">Jump to search</a>
		<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">Method of espionage tradecraft</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For a USB device used as a public dead drop, see <a href="/wiki/USB_dead_drop" title="USB dead drop">USB dead drop</a>.</div>
<p>A <b>dead drop</b> or <b>dead letter box</b> is a method of <a href="/wiki/Espionage" title="Espionage">espionage</a> <a href="/wiki/Tradecraft" title="Tradecraft">tradecraft</a> used to pass items or information between two individuals (e.g., a <a href="/wiki/Agent_handling#Case_officer" title="Agent handling">case officer</a> and an <a href="/wiki/Espionage#Agents_in_espionage" title="Espionage">agent</a>, or two agents) using a secret location. By avoiding direct meetings, individuals can maintain <a href="/wiki/Operational_security" class="mw-redirect" title="Operational security">operational security</a>.  This method stands in contrast to the <b>live drop</b>, so-called because two persons meet to exchange items or information.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Espionage" title="Espionage">Spies</a> and their handlers have been known to perform dead drops using various techniques to hide items (such as money, secrets or instructions) and to signal that the drop has been made. Although the signal and location by necessity must be agreed upon in advance, the signal may or may not be located close to the dead drop itself. The operatives may not necessarily know one another or ever meet.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Considerations">Considerations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dead_drop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Considerations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The location and nature of the dead drop must enable retrieval of the hidden item without the operatives being spotted by a member of the public, the police, or other security forces—therefore, common everyday items and behavior are used to avoid arousing suspicion. Any hidden location could serve, although often a <a href="/wiki/Cut-out_(espionage)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cut-out (espionage)">cut-out</a> device is used, such as a loose brick in a wall, a (cut-out) library book, or a hole in a tree. 
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg/220px-%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg/330px-%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg/440px-%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4992" data-file-height="3328" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:%E2%80%9CDead%E2%80%9D_Drop_Spike_-_Flickr_-_The_Central_Intelligence_Agency.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Dead drop spike</div></div></div>
<p>A <i>dead drop spike</i> is a <a href="/wiki/Concealment_device" title="Concealment device">concealment device</a> similar to a <a href="/wiki/Geocaching#Size_classifications" title="Geocaching">microcache</a>. It has been used since the late 1960s to hide money, maps, documents, <a href="/wiki/Microfilm" class="mw-redirect" title="Microfilm">microfilm</a>, and other items. The spike is water- and <a href="/wiki/Mildew" title="Mildew">mildew</a>-proof and can be pushed into the ground or placed in a shallow stream to be retrieved at a later time.
</p><p>Signaling devices can include a <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalk</a> mark on a wall, a piece of <a href="/wiki/Chewing_gum" title="Chewing gum">chewing gum</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Street_light" title="Street light">lamppost</a>, or a <a href="/wiki/Newspaper" title="Newspaper">newspaper</a> left on a <a href="/wiki/Bench_(furniture)" title="Bench (furniture)">park bench</a>. Alternatively, the signal can be made from inside the agent's own home, by, for example, hanging a distinctively-colored towel from a balcony, or placing a potted plant on a window sill where it is visible to anyone on the street.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Drawbacks">Drawbacks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dead_drop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Drawbacks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While the dead drop method is useful in preventing the instantaneous capture of either an operative/<a href="/wiki/Handler_(spying)" class="mw-redirect" title="Handler (spying)">handler</a> pair or an entire espionage network, it is not without disadvantages. If one of the operatives is compromised, they may reveal the location and signal for that specific dead drop. <a href="/wiki/Counterintelligence" title="Counterintelligence">Counterintelligence</a> can then use the dead drop as a <a href="/wiki/Double_agent" title="Double agent">double agent</a> for a variety of purposes, such as to feed <a href="/wiki/Misinformation" title="Misinformation">misinformation</a> to the enemy or to identify other operatives using it or ultimately to <a href="/wiki/Booby_trap" title="Booby trap">booby trap</a> it.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> There is also the risk that a third party may find the material deposited.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Modern_techniques">Modern techniques</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dead_drop&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Modern techniques">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"/><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Short-range_agent_communications" title="Short-range agent communications">Short-range agent communications</a></div>
<p>On January 23, 2006, the Russian <a href="/wiki/Federal_Security_Service_(Russia)" class="mw-redirect" title="Federal Security Service (Russia)">FSB</a> accused <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Foreign relations of the United Kingdom">Britain</a> of using <a href="/wiki/Wireless_communication" class="mw-redirect" title="Wireless communication">wireless</a> dead drops concealed inside hollowed-out rocks ("spy rock") to collect espionage information from agents in Russia. According to the Russian authorities, the agent delivering information would approach the rock and transmit data wirelessly into it from a hand-held device, and later, his British handlers would pick up the stored data by similar means.<sup id="cite_ref-Guardian_spies_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guardian_spies-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/SecureDrop" title="SecureDrop">SecureDrop</a>, initially called DeadDrop, is a software suite for teams that allows them to create a digital dead drop location to receive tips from whistleblowers through the Internet. The team members and whistleblowers never communicate directly and never know each other's identity, therefore allowing whistleblowers to dead-drop information despite the <a href="/wiki/Mass_surveillance" title="Mass surveillance">mass surveillance</a> and privacy violations which had become commonplace in the beginning of the twenty-first century.
</p>
