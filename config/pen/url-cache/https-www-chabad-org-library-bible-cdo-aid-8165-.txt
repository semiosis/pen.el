<h3>Chapter 1</h3>
<table width="100%" class="Co_TanachTable" border="0" cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0">
<tr class="Co_Verse">
<td valign="top"><a id="v8177"></a><a id="v1" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v1">1</a><span class="co_VerseText">In the beginning of God's creation of the heavens and the earth.</span></td>
<td class="Co_Spacer"> </td>
<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><a id="v1" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v1">א</a><span class="co_VerseText">בְּרֵאשִׁ֖ית בָּרָ֣א אֱלֹהִ֑ים אֵ֥ת הַשָּׁמַ֖יִם וְאֵ֥ת הָאָֽרֶץ:</span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Rashi">
<td valign="top"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>In the beginning.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">
Said Rabbi Isaac: It was not necessary to begin the Torah except from “This month is to you,” (<a href="/9873#v2">Exod. 12:2)</a> which is the first commandment that the Israelites were commanded, (for the main purpose of the Torah is its commandments, and although several commandments are found in Genesis, e.g., circumcision and the prohibition of eating the thigh sinew, they could have been included together with the other commandments). Now for what reason did He commence with “In the beginning?” Because of [the verse] “The strength of His works He related to His people, to give them the inheritance of the nations” (<a href="/16332#v6">Ps. 111:6)</a>. For if the nations of the world should say to Israel, “You are robbers, for you conquered by force the lands of the seven nations [of Canaan],” they will reply, "The entire earth belongs to the Holy One, blessed be He; He created it (this we learn from the story of the Creation) and gave it to whomever He deemed proper When He wished, He gave it to them, and when He wished, He took it away from them and gave it to us.</span></span></td>

<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>בְּרֵאשִׁית.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">
אָמַר רַבִּי יִצְחָק לֹֹֹֹֹא הָיָה צָרִיךְ לְהַתְחִיל אֶת הַתּוֹרָה אֶלָּא מֵהַחֹדֶשׁ הַזֶּה לָכֶם, שֶׁהִיא מִצְוָה רִאשׁוֹנָה שֶׁנִּצְטַוּוּ בָּהּ יִשׂרָאֵל, וּמַה טַּעַם פָּתַח בִּבְרֵאשִׁית? מִשׁוּם כֹּחַ מַעֲשָׂיו הִגִּיד לְעַמּוֹ לָתֵת לָהֶם נַחֲלַת גּוֹיִם (תהילים קי"א), שֶׁאִם יֹאמְרוּ אֻמּוֹת הָעוֹלָם לְיִשְׁרָאֵל לִסְטִים אַתֶּם, שֶׁכְּבַשְׁתֶּם אַרְצוֹת שִׁבְעָה גוֹיִם, הֵם אוֹמְרִים לָהֶם כָּל הָאָרֶץ שֶׁל הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא הִיא, הוּא בְרָאָהּ וּנְתָנָהּ לַאֲשֶׁר יָשַׁר בְּעֵינָיו, בִּרְצוֹנוֹ נְתָנָהּ לָהֶם, וּבִרְצוֹנוֹ נְטָלָהּ מֵהֶם וּנְתָנָהּ לָנוּ:
</span></span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Rashi">
<td valign="top"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>In the beginning of God’s creation of.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">Heb. <span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">בְּרֵאשִית בָּרָא</span>. This verse calls for a midrashic interpretation [because according to its simple interpretation, the vowelization of the word <span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">בָּרָא</span>, should be different, as Rashi explains further]. It teaches us that the sequence of the Creation as written is impossible, as is written immediately below] as our Rabbis stated (<em>Letters of R. Akiva</em> , letter “beth” ; <em>Gen. Rabbah</em> 1:6; <em>Lev. Rabbah</em> 36:4): [God created the world] for the sake of the Torah, which is called (<a href="/16379#v22">Prov. 8:22)</a>: “the beginning of His way,” and for the sake of Israel, who are called (<a href="/15999#v3">Jer. 2:3)</a> “the first of His grain.” But if you wish to explain it according to its simple meaning, explain it thus: “At the beginning of the creation of heaven and earth, the earth was astonishing with emptiness, and darkness…and God said, ‘Let there be light.’” But Scripture did not come to teach the sequence of the Creation, to say that these came first, for if it came to teach this, it should have written:“At first (<span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">בָּרִאשׁוֹנָה</span>) He created the heavens and the earth,” for there is no <span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">רֵאשִׁית</span> in Scripture that is not connected to the following word, [i.e., in the construct state] like (ibid. 27:1):“In the beginning of (<span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">בְּרֵאשִית</span>) the reign of Jehoiakim” ; (below 10:10)“the beginning of (<span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">רֵאשִׁית</span>) his reign” ; (<a href="/9982#v4">Deut. 18:4)</a>“the first (<span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">רֵאשִׁית</span>) of your corn.” Here too, you say <span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">בְּרֵאשִׁית בָּרָא אלֹהִים</span>, like <span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">בְּרֵאשִׁית בְּרֹא</span>, in the beginning of creating. And similar to this is,“At the beginning of the Lord’s speaking (<span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">דִּבֶּר</span>) to Hosea,” (<a href="/16155#v2">Hos. 1:2)</a>, i.e., at the beginning of the speaking (<span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">דִּבּוּרוֹ</span>) of the Holy One, Blessed be He, to Hosea, “the Lord said to Hosea, etc.” Now if you say that it came to teach that these (i.e., heaven and earth) were created first, and that its meaning is: In the beginning of all, He created these-and that there are elliptical verses that omit one word, like (<a href="/16405#v10">Job 3:10)</a>: “For [He] did not shut the doors of my [mother’s] womb,” and it does not explain who it was who shut [the womb]; and like (<a href="/15939#v4">Isa. 8:4)</a>: “he will carry off the wealth of Damascus,” and it does not explain who will carry it off; and like (<a href="/16178#v12">Amos 6:12)</a>: “or will one plow with cattle,” and it does not explain: “if a man will plow with cattle” ; and like (Isa. 46: 10): “telling the end from the beginning,” and it does not explain that [it means] telling the end of a matter from the beginning of a matter-if so, [if you say that Scripture indicates the order of creation] be astounded at yourself, for the water preceded, as it is written: “and the spirit of God hovered over the face of the water,” and Scripture did not yet disclose when the creation of water took place! From this you learn that the water preceded the earth. Moreover, the heavens were created from fire and water. Perforce, you must admit that Scripture did not teach us anything about the sequence of the earlier and the later [acts of creation].</span></span></td>

<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>בְּרֵאשִׁית בָּרָא.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">
אֵין הַמִּקְרָא הַזֶּה אוֹמֵר אֶלָּא דָּרְשֵׁנִי, כְּמוֹ שֶׁדְּרָשׁוּהוּ רַבּוֹתֵינוּ בִּשְׁבִיל הַתּוֹרָה שֶׁנִקְרֵאת רֵאשִׁית דַּרְכּוֹ (משלי ח'), וּבִשְׁבִיל יִשְׂרָאֵל שֶׁנִקְרְאוּ רֵאשִׁית תְּבוּאָתוֹ (ירמיה ב'); וְאִם בָּאתָ לְפָרְשׁוֹ כִּפְשׁוּטוֹ, כָּךְ פָּרְשֵׁהוּ בְּרֵאשִׁית בְּרִיאַת שָׁמַיִם וָאָרֶץ, וְהָאָרֶץ הָיְתָה תֹהוּ וָבֹהוּ וְחֹשֶׁךְ וַיֹּאמֶר אֱלֹהִים יְהִי אוֹר וְלֹא בָא הַמִּקְרָא לְהוֹרוֹת סֵדֶר הַבְּרִיאָה לוֹמַר שֶׁאֵלּוּ קָדְמוּ, שֶׁאִם בָּא לְהוֹרוֹת כָּךְ, הָיָה לוֹ לִכְתֹּב בָּרִאשׁוֹנָה בָּרָא אֶת הַשָּׁמַיִם וְגוֹ' שֶׁאֵין לְךָ רֵאשִׁית בַּמִּקְרָא שֶׁאֵינוֹ דָבוּק לַתֵּבָה שֶׁלְּאַחֲרָיו, כְּמוֹ בְּרֵאשִׁית מַמְלֶכֶת יְהוֹיָקִים (שׁם כ"ז), רֵאשִׁית מַמְלַכְתּוֹ (בראשית י'), רֵאשִׁית דְּגָנְךָ (דברים י"ח, ד'), – אַף כָּאן אַתָּה אוֹמֵר בְּרֵאשִׁית בָּרָא אֱלֹהִים וְגוֹ', כְּמוֹ בְּרֵאשִׁית בְּרֹא; וְדוֹמֶה לוֹ תְּחִלַּת דִּבֶּר ה' בְּהוֹשֵׁעַ (הושע א'), כְּלוֹמַר תְּחִלַת דִּבּוּרוֹ שֶׁל הַקָּדוֹשׁ בָּרוּךְ הוּא בְּהוֹשֵׁעַ, וַיֹּאמֶר ה' אֶל הוֹשֵׁעַ וְגוֹ'. וְאִם תֹּאמַר לְהוֹרוֹת בָּא שֶׁאֵלּוּ תְּחִלָּה נִבְרְאוּ, וּפֵרוּשׁוֹ בְּרֵאשִׁית הַכֹּל בָּרָא אֵלוּ – וְֵישׁ לְךָ מִקְרָאוֹת שֶׁמְּקַצְּרִים לְשׁוֹנָם וּמְמַעֲטִים תֵּבָה אַחַת, כְּמוֹ כִּי לֹא סָגַר דַּלְתֵי בִטְנִי (איוב ג'), וְלֹא פֵּרֵשׁ מִי הַסּוֹגֵר וּכְמוֹ יִשָּׂא אֶת חֵיל דַּמֶּשֶׂק (ישעיהו ח'), וְלֹא פֵּרֵשׁ מִי יִשָּׂאֶנּוּ; וּכְמוֹ אִם יַחֲרוֹשׁ בַּבְּקָרִים (עמוס ו'), וְלֹא פֵּרֵשׁ אִם יַחֲרוֹשׁ אָדָם בַּבְּקָרִים, וּכְמוֹ מַגִּיד מֵרֵאשִׁית אַחֲרִית (ישעיהו מ"ו), וְלֹא פֵּרֵשׁ מַגִּיד מֵרֵאשִׁית דָּבָר אַחֲרִית דָּבָר – אִם כֵּן, תְּמַהּ עַל עַצְמְךָ, שֶׁהֲרֵי הַמַּיִם קָדְמוּ, שֶׁהֲרֵי כְתִיב וְרוּחַ אֱלֹהִים מְרַחֶפֶת עַל פְּנֵי הַמָּיִם, וְעַדַיִין לֹא גִלָּה הַמִּקְרָא, בְּרִיאַת הַמַּיִם מָתַי הָיְתָה, הָא לָמַדְתָּ, שֶׁקָּדְמוּ הַמַּיִם לָאָרֶץ, וְעוֹד, שֶׁהַשָּׁמַיִם מֵאֵשׁ וּמַיִם נִבְרְאוּ, עַל כָּרְחֲךָ לֹא לִמֵּד הַמִּקְרָא סֵדֶר הַמֻּקְדָמִים וְהַמְאֻחָרִים כְּלוּם:

<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><a id="v28" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v28">כח</a><span class="co_VerseText">וַיְבָ֣רֶךְ אֹתָם֘ אֱלֹהִים֒ וַיֹּ֨אמֶר לָהֶ֜ם אֱלֹהִ֗ים פְּר֥וּ וּרְב֛וּ וּמִלְא֥וּ אֶת־הָאָ֖רֶץ וְכִבְשֻׁ֑הָ וּרְד֞וּ בִּדְגַ֤ת הַיָּם֙ וּבְע֣וֹף הַשָּׁמַ֔יִם וּבְכָל־חַיָּ֖ה הָֽרֹמֶ֥שֶׂת עַל־הָאָֽרֶץ:</span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Rashi">
<td valign="top"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>and subdue it.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">The “vav” [in <span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">וְכִבְשֻׁהָ</span>] is missing, [allowing the word to be read <span class="torah-heb" dir="rtl">וְכִבְשָׁה</span>, the masculine singular imperative] to teach you that the male subdues the female that she should not be a gadabout (<em>Gen. Rabbah</em> 8:12), and it is also meant to teach you that the man, whose way it is to subdue, is commanded to propagate, but not the woman (<em>Yev.</em> Yev. 65b).</span></span></td>
<td class="Co_Spacer"> </td>
<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>וְכִבְשֻׁהָ.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">
חָסֵר וָי"ו לְלַמֶּדְךָ שֶׁהַזָּכָר כּוֹבֵשׁ אֶת הַנְּקֵבָה, שֶׁלֹּא תְהֵא יַצְאָנִית, וְעוֹד לְלַמֶּדְךָ שֶׁהָאִישׁ שֶׁדַּרְכּוֹ לִכְבֹּשׁ מְצֻוֶּה עַל פְּרִיָּה וּרְבִיָּה וְלֹא הָאִשָּׁה:
</span></span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Verse">
<td valign="top"><a id="v8205"></a><a id="v29" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v29">29</a><span class="co_VerseText">And God said, "Behold, I have given you every seed bearing herb, which is upon the surface of the entire earth, and every tree that has seed bearing fruit; it will be yours for food.</span></td>
<td class="Co_Spacer"> </td>
<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><a id="v29" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v29">כט</a><span class="co_VerseText">וַיֹּ֣אמֶר אֱלֹהִ֗ים הִנֵּה֩ נָתַ֨תִּי לָכֶ֜ם אֶת־כָּל־עֵ֣שֶׂב | זֹרֵ֣עַ זֶ֗רַע אֲשֶׁר֙ עַל־פְּנֵ֣י כָל־הָאָ֔רֶץ וְאֶת־כָּל־הָעֵ֛ץ אֲשֶׁר־בּ֥וֹ פְרִי־עֵ֖ץ זֹרֵ֣עַ זָ֑רַע לָכֶ֥ם יִֽהְיֶ֖ה לְאָכְלָֽה:</span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Verse">
<td valign="top"><a id="v8206"></a><a id="v30" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v30">30</a><span class="co_VerseText">And to all the beasts of the earth and to all the fowl of the heavens, and to everything that moves upon the earth, in which there is a living spirit, every green herb to eat," and it was so.</span></td>
<td class="Co_Spacer"> </td>
<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><a id="v30" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v30">ל</a><span class="co_VerseText">וּלְכָל־חַיַּ֣ת הָ֠אָ֠רֶץ וּלְכָל־ע֨וֹף הַשָּׁמַ֜יִם וּלְכֹ֣ל | רוֹמֵ֣שׂ עַל־הָאָ֗רֶץ אֲשֶׁר־בּוֹ֙ נֶ֣פֶשׁ חַיָּ֔ה אֶת־כָּל־יֶ֥רֶק עֵ֖שֶׂב לְאָכְלָ֑ה וַֽיְהִי־כֵֽן:</span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Rashi">
<td valign="top"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>it will be yours for food. And to all the beasts of the earth.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">He equated cattle and the beasts to them [to man] regarding the food [that they were permitted to eat]. He did not permit Adam and his wife to kill a creature and to eat its flesh; only every green herb they were all permitted to eat equally. When the sons of Noah came, He permitted them to eat flesh, as it is said (below 9:3): “Every creeping thing that is alive, etc.” Like the green herbs, which I permitted to the first man, I have given you everything. — [from <em>Sanh.</em> 59b]</span></span></td>
<td class="Co_Spacer"> </td>
<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>לכם יהיה לְאָכְלָה וּלְכָל־חַיַּת הָאָרֶץ.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">
הִשְׁוָה לָהֶם בְּהֵמוֹת וְחַיּוֹת לְמַאֲכָל, וְלֹא הִרְשָׁה לְאָדָם וּלְאִשְׁתּוֹ לְהָמִית בְּרִיָּה וְלֶאֱכֹל בָּשָׂר, אַךְ כָּל יֶרֶק עֵשֶׂב יֹאכְלוּ יַחַד כֻּלָם, וּכְשֶׁבָּאוּ בְנֵי נֹחַ הִתִּיר לָהֶם בָּשָׂר, שֶׁנֶאֱמַר כָּל רֶמֶשׂ אֲשֶׁר הוּא חַי וְגוֹ' כְּיֶרֶק עֵשֶׂב שֶׁהִתַּרְתִּי לְאָדָם הָרִאשׁוֹן נָתַתִּי לָכֶם אֶת כֹּל:
</span></span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Verse">
<td valign="top"><a id="v8207"></a><a id="v31" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v31">31</a><span class="co_VerseText">And God saw all that He had made, and behold it was very good, and it was evening and it was morning, the sixth day.</span></td>
<td class="Co_Spacer"> </td>
<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><a id="v31" class="co_VerseNum" href="#v31">לא</a><span class="co_VerseText">וַיַּ֤רְא אֱלֹהִים֙ אֶת־כָּל־אֲשֶׁ֣ר עָשָׂ֔ה וְהִנֵּה־ט֖וֹב מְאֹ֑ד וַֽיְהִי־עֶ֥רֶב וַֽיְהִי־בֹ֖קֶר י֥וֹם הַשִּׁשִּֽׁי:</span></td>
</tr>
<tr class="Co_Rashi">
<td valign="top"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>the sixth day.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">Scripture added a “hey” on the sixth [day], at the completion of the Creation, to tell us that He stipulated with them, [“you were created] on the condition that Israel accept the Five Books of the Torah.” [The numerical value of the “hey” is five.] (<em>Tanchuma Bereishith</em> 1). Another explanation for “the sixth day” : They [the works of creation] were all suspended until the “sixth day,” referring to the sixth day of Sivan, which was prepared for the giving of the Torah (<em>Shab.</em> 88a). [The“hey” is the definite article, alluding to the well-known sixth day, the sixth day of Sivan, when the Torah was given (ad loc.).]</span></span></td>
<td class="Co_Spacer"> </td>
<td valign="top" class="hebrew"><span class="Co_RashiContainer"><span class="co_RashiTitle"><co:TanachRashiTitle>יוֹם הַשִּׁשִּֽׁי.</co:TanachRashiTitle> </span><span class="co_RashiText">
הוֹסִיף ה' בַּשִּׁשִּׁי בִגְמַר מַעֲשֵׂה בְרֵאשִׁית, לוֹמַר שֶׁהִתְנָה עִמָּהֶם, עַל מְנָת שֶׁיְּקַבְּלוּ עֲלֵיהֶם יִשְׂרָאֵל חֲמִשָּׁה חֻמְּשֵׁי תוֹרָה. דָּבָר אַחֵר יוֹם הַשִּׁשִּׁי, כֻּלָּם תְּלוּיִם וְעוֹמְדִים עַד יוֹם הַשִּׁשִּׁי, הוּא ו' בְּסִיוָן הַמּוּכָן לְמַתַּן תּוֹרָה:
</span></span></td>
</tr>
