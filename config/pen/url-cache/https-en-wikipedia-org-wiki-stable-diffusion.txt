<p><b>Stable Diffusion</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning model</a> developed by StabilityAI, in collaboration with EleutherAI and LAION,<sup id="cite_ref-stable-diffusion-launch_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stable-diffusion-launch-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> to generate digital images from <a href="/wiki/Natural_language" title="Natural language">natural language</a> descriptions. The model can be used for other tasks too, like generating image-to-image translations guided by a text prompt.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It can run on most consumer hardware equipped with a modest <a href="/wiki/GPU" class="mw-redirect" title="GPU">GPU</a> and was hailed by <i><a href="/wiki/PC_World" title="PC World">PC World</a></i> as "the next <a href="/wiki/Killer_app" class="mw-redirect" title="Killer app">killer app</a> for your PC".<sup id="cite_ref-pcworld_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcworld-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stability AI, the company behind Stable Diffusion, is in talks to raise up to one billion dollars in valuation as of September 2022.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="License">License</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stable_Diffusion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: License">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Unlike competing models like <a href="/wiki/DALL-E" title="DALL-E">DALL-E</a>, Stable Diffusion makes its <a href="/wiki/Source-available_software" title="Source-available software">source code available</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-stability_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stability-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Its license prohibits certain harmful use cases.<sup id="cite_ref-washingtonpost_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Critics have raised concerns about <a href="/wiki/AI_ethics" class="mw-redirect" title="AI ethics">AI ethics</a>, stating that the model can be used to create <a href="/wiki/Deepfake" title="Deepfake">deepfakes</a><sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and also questioning the legality of generating images with a model trained on a dataset containing copyrighted content without the consent of the original artists.<sup id="cite_ref-kotaku_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kotaku-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Training">Training</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Stable_Diffusion&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Training">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Stable Diffusion was trained on a subset of the LAION-Aesthetics V2 dataset.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> It was trained using 256 <a href="/wiki/Ampere_(microarchitecture)" title="Ampere (microarchitecture)">Nvidia A100</a> GPUs at a cost of $600,000.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
