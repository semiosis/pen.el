<p>THE EPISTLE OF POLYCARP<br>
Translated by J.B. Lightfoot. </p>

<p>Polycarp prologue:1<br>
Polycarp and the presbyters that are with him unto the Church of God<br>
which sojourneth at Philippi; mercy unto you and peace from God<br>
Almighty and Jesus Christ our Savior be multiplied.</p>

<p>Polycarp 1:1<br>
 I rejoiced with you greatly in our Lord Jesus Christ, for that ye<br>
received the followers of the true Love and escorted them on their<br>
way, as befitted you--those men encircled in saintly bonds which are<br>
the diadems of them that be truly chosen of God and our Lord;</p>

<p>Polycarp 1:2<br>
and that the steadfast root of your faith which was famed from<br>
primitive times abideth until now and beareth fruit unto our Lord<br>
Jesus Christ, who endured to face even death for our sins, <I>whom God<br>
raised, having loosed the pangs of Hades; on whom,</I></p>

<p>Polycarp 1:3<br>
<I>though ye saw Him not, ye believe with joy unutterable and full of<br>
glory</I>; unto which joy many desire to enter in; forasmuch as ye know<br>
that it is <I>by grace ye are saved, not of works</I>, but by the will of<br>
God through Jesus Christ.</p>

<p>Polycarp 2:1<br>
 <I>Wherefore gird up your loins and serve God in fear</I> and truth,<br>
forsaking the vain and empty talking and the error of the many, <I>for<br>
that ye have believed on Him that raised our Lord Jesus Christ from<br>
the dead and gave unto him glory</I> and a throne on His right hand;<br>
unto whom all things were made subject that are in heaven and that<br>
are on the earth; to whom every creature that hath breath doeth<br>
service; who cometh as <I>judge of quick and dead</I>; whose blood God<br>
will require of them that are disobedient unto Him.</p>

<p>Polycarp 2:2<br>
Now <I>He that raised Him</I> from the dead <I>will raise us also</I>; if we<br>
do His will and walk in His commandments and love the things which He<br>
loved, abstaining from all unrighteousness, covetousness, love of<br>
money, evil speaking, false witness; <I>not rendering evil for evil or<br>
railing for railing</I> or blow for blow or cursing for cursing;</p>

<p>Polycarp 2:3<br>
but remembering the words which the Lord spake, as He taught; <I>Judge<br>
not that ye be not judged. Forgive, and it shall be forgiven to<br>
you. Have mercy that ye may receive mercy. With what measure ye<br>
mete, it shall be measured to you again;</I> and again <I>Blessed are<br>
the poor and they that are persecuted for righteousness' sake, for<br>
theirs is the kingdom of God</I>.</p>

<p>Polycarp 3:1<br>
 These things, brethren, I write unto you concerning righteousness,<br>
not because I laid this charge upon myself, but because ye invited<br>
me.</p>

<p>Polycarp 3:2<br>
For neither am I, nor is any other like unto me, able to follow the<br>
wisdom of the blessed and glorious Paul, who when he came among you<br>
taught face to face with the men of that day the word which<br>
concerneth truth carefully and surely; who also, when he was absent,<br>
wrote a letter unto you, into the which if ye look diligently, ye<br>
shall be able to be builded up unto the faith given to you,</p>

<p>Polycarp 3:3<br>
<I>which is the mother of us all</I>, while hope followeth after and love<br>
goeth before--love toward God and Christ and toward our neighbor. For<br>
if any man be occupied with these, he hath fulfilled the commandment<br>
of righteousness; for he that hath love is far from all sin.</p>

<p>Polycarp 4:1<br>
 <I>But the love of money is the beginning of all troubles</I>. Knowing<br>
therefore that <I>we brought nothing into the world neither can we<br>
carry anything out</I>, let us arm ourselves with the armor of<br>
righteousness, and let us teach ourselves first to walk in the<br>
commandment of the Lord;</p>

<p>Polycarp 4:2<br>
and then our wives also, to walk in the faith that hath been given<br>
unto them and in love and purity, cherishing their own husbands in<br>
all truth and loving all men equally in all chastity, and to train<br>
their children in the training of the fear of God.</p>

<p>Polycarp 4:3<br>
Our widows must be sober-minded as touching the faith of the Lord,<br>
making intercession without ceasing for all men, abstaining from all<br>
calumny, evil speaking, false witness, love of money, and every evil<br>
thing, knowing that they are God's altar, and that all sacrifices are<br>
carefully inspected, and nothing escapeth Him either of their<br>
thoughts or intents or any of the secret things of the heart.</p>

<p>Polycarp 5:1<br>
 Knowing then that <I>God is not mocked</I>, we ought to walk worthily of<br>
His commandment and His glory.</p>

<p>Polycarp 5:2<br>
In like manner deacons should be blameless in the presence of His<br>
righteousness, as deacons of God and Christ and not of men; not<br>
calumniators, not double-tongued, not lovers of money, temperate in<br>
all things, compassionate, diligent, walking according to the truth<br>
of the Lord who became a <I>minister (deacon) of all</I>. For if we be<br>
well pleasing unto Him in this present world, we shall receive the<br>
future world also, according as He promised us to raise us from the<br>
dead, and that if we conduct ourselves worthily of Him <I>we shall<br>
also reign with Him,</I> if indeed we have faith.</p>

<p>Polycarp 5:3<br>
In like manner also the younger men must be blameless in all things,<br>
caring for purity before everything and curbing themselves from every<br>
evil. For it is a good thing to refrain from lusts in the world, for<br>
every <I>lust warreth against the Spirit</I>, and <I>neither whoremongers<br>
nor effeminate persons nor defilers of themselves with men shall<br>
inherit the kingdom of God</I>, neither they that do untoward things.<br>
Wherefore it is right to abstain from all these things, submitting<br>
yourselves to the presbyters and deacons as to God and Christ. The<br>
virgins must walk in a blameless and pure conscience.</p>

<p>Polycarp 6:1<br>
 And the presbyters also must be compassionate, merciful towards all<br>
men, <I>turning back the sheep that are gone astray, visiting all the<br>
infirm, not neglecting a widow or an orphan or a poor man: but<br>
providing always for that which is honorable in the sight of God<br>
and of men</I>, abstaining from all anger, respect of persons,<br>
unrighteous judgment, being far from all love of money, not quick to<br>
believe anything against any man, not hasty in judgment, knowing that<br>
we all are debtors of sin.</p>

<p>Polycarp 6:2<br>
If then we entreat the Lord that He would forgive us, we also ought<br>
to forgive: for we are before the eyes of our Lord and God, and we<br>
must <I>all stand at the judgment-seat of Christ</I>, and <I>each man must<br>
give an account of himself</I>.</p>

<p>Polycarp 6:3<br>
Let us therefore so serve Him with fear and all reverence, as He<br>
himself gave commandment and the Apostles who preached the Gospel to<br>
us and the prophets who proclaimed beforehand the coming of our Lord;<br>
being zealous as touching that which is good, abstaining from<br>
offenses and from the false brethren and from them that bear the name<br>
of the Lord in hypocrisy, who lead foolish men astray.</p>

<p>Polycarp 7:1<br>
 For every one <I>who shall not confess that Jesus Christ is come in<br>
the flesh, is antichrist</I>: and whosoever shall not confess the<br>
testimony of the Cross, is of the devil; and whosoever shall pervert<br>
the oracles of the Lord to his own lusts and say that there is<br>
neither resurrection nor judgment, that man is the firstborn of<br>
Satan.</p>

<p>Polycarp 7:2<br>
Wherefore let us forsake the vain doing of the many and their false<br>
teachings, and turn unto the word which was delivered unto us from<br>
the beginning, <I>being sober unto prayer</I> and constant in fastings,<br>
entreating the all-seeing God with supplications that <I>He bring us<br>
not into temptation</I>, according as the Lord said, <I>The Spirit is<br>
indeed willing, but the flesh is weak</I>.</p>

<p>Polycarp 8:1<br>
 Let us therefore without ceasing hold fast by our hope and by the<br>
earnest of our righteousness, which is Jesus Christ who <I>took up our<br>
sins in His own body upon the tree, who did no sin, neither was<br>
guile found in His mouth</I>, but for our sakes He endured all things,<br>
that we might live in Him.</p>

<p>Polycarp 8:2<br>
Let us therefore become imitators of His endurance; and if we should<br>
suffer for His name's sake, let us glorify Him. For He gave this<br>
example to us in His own person, and we believed this.</p>

<p>Polycarp 9:1<br>
 I exhort you all therefore to be obedient unto the word of<br>
righteousness and to practice all endurance, which also ye saw with<br>
your own eyes in the blessed Ignatius and Zosimus and Rufus, yea and<br>
in others also who came from among yourselves, as well as in Paul<br>
himself and the rest of the Apostles;</p>

<p>Polycarp 9:2<br>
being persuaded that all these <I>ran not in vain</I> but in faith and<br>
righteousness, and that they are in their due place in the presence<br>
of the Lord, with whom also they suffered. For they <I>loved not the<br>
present world</I>, but Him that died for our sakes and was raised by<br>
God for us.</p>

<p>Polycarp 10:1<br>
 Stand fast therefore in these things and follow the example of the<br>
Lord, <I>being firm in the faith</I> and <I>immovable, in love of the<br>
brotherhood kindly affectioned one to another</I>, partners with the<br>
truth, <I>forestalling one another</I> in the gentleness of the Lord,<br>
despising no man.</p>

<p>Polycarp 10:2<br>
<I>When ye are able to do good</I>, defer it not, for <I>Pitifulness<br>
delivereth from death. Be ye all subject one to another, having<br>
your conversation</I> unblamable <I>among the gentiles, that your good<br>
works</I> both ye may receive praise and the Lord may not be<br>
blasphemed in you.</p>

<p>Polycarp 10:3<br>
But <I>woe to him through whom the name of the Lord be blasphemed</I>.<br>
Therefore teach all men soberness, in which ye yourselves also walk.</p>

<p>Polycarp 11:1<br>
 I was exceedingly grieved for Valens, who aforetime was a presbyter<br>
among you, because he is so ignorant of the office which was given<br>
unto him. I warn you therefore that ye refrain from covetousness, and<br>
that ye be pure and truthful. Refrain from all evil.</p>

<p>Polycarp 11:2<br>
But he who cannot govern himself in these things, how doth he enjoin<br>
this upon another? If a man refrain not from covetousness, he shall<br>
be defiled by idolatry, and shall be judged as one of the Gentiles<br>
who <I>know not the judgment of the Lord, Nay, know we not, that the<br>
saints shall judge the world</I>, as Paul teacheth?</p>

<p>Polycarp 11:3<br>
But I have not found any such thing in you, neither have heard<br>
thereof, among whom the blessed Paul labored, who were his <br>
<I>letters</I> in the beginning. For <I>he boasteth of you in</I> all those<br>
<I>churches</I> which alone at that time knew God; for we knew Him not as<br>
yet.</p>

<p>Polycarp 11:4<br>
Therefore I am exceedingly grieved for him and for his wife, unto<br>
whom may the Lord grant true repentance. Be ye therefore yourselves<br>
also sober herein, and <I>hold not such as enemies</I> but restore them<br>
as frail and erring members, that ye may save the whole body of you.<br>
For so doing, ye do edify one another.</p>

<p>Polycarp 12:1<br>
 For I am persuaded that ye are well trained in the sacred writings,<br>
and nothing is hidden from you. But to myself this is not granted.<br>
Only, as it is said in these scriptures, <I>Be ye angry and sin not</I>,<br>
and <I>Let not the sun set on your wrath</I>. Blessed is he that<br>
remembereth this; and I trust that this is in you.</p>

<p>Polycarp 12:2<br>
Now may the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, and the eternal<br>
High-priest Himself the [Son of God Jesus Christ, build you up in<br>
faith and truth, and in all gentleness and in all avoidance of wrath<br>
and in forbearance and long suffering and in patient endurance and in<br>
purity; and may He grant unto you a lot and portion among His saints,<br>
and to us with you, and to all that are under heaven, who shall<br>
believe on our Lord and God Jesus Christ and on His Father <I>that<br>
raised him from the dead.</I></p>

<p>Polycarp 12:3<br>
<I>Pray for all the saints</I>. Pray also <I>for kings</I> and powers and<br>
princes and <I>for them that persecute</I> and hate <I>you</I> and for <I>the<br>
enemies of the cross</I>, that your fruit may be <I>manifest among all<br>
men</I>, that ye may be perfect in Him.</p>

<p>Polycarp 13:1<br>
Ye wrote to me, both ye yourselves and Ignatius, asking that if any<br>
one should go to Syria he might carry thither the letters from you.<br>
And this I will do, if I get a fit opportunity, either I myself, or<br>
he whom I shall send to be ambassador on your behalf also.</p>

<p>Polycarp 13:2<br>
The letters of Ignatius which were sent to us by him, and others as<br>
many as we had by us, we send unto you, according as ye gave charge;<br>
the which are subjoined to this letter; from which ye will be able to<br>
gain great advantage. For they comprise faith and endurance and every<br>
kind of edification, which pertaineth unto our Lord. Moreover<br>
concerning Ignatius himself and those that were with him, if ye have<br>
any sure tidings, certify us.</p>

<p>Polycarp 14:1<br>
I write these things to you by Crescens, whom I commended to you<br>
recently and now commend unto you: for he hath walked blamelessly<br>
with us; and I believe also with you in like manner. But ye shall<br>
have his sister commended, when she shall come to you. Fare ye well<br>
in the Lord Jesus Christ in grace, ye and all yours. Amen.</p>
